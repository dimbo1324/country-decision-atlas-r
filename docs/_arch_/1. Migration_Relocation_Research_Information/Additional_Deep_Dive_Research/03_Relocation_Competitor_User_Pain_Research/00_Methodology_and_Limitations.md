# Methodology and Limitations

**Research date:** 2026-06-17  
**Scope:** user pains, competitor landscape, platform functionality and monetisation in the relocation/country-choice market.

## Evidence tiers

### Tier 1 — High-confidence factual sources
Official or primary sources: government portals, official platform pages, company pricing pages, institutional datasets and official documentation. Examples: Numbeo API pricing, Remote pricing, Deel pricing, InterNations support pages, LegiScan pricing, OECD Better Life Index, company product pages.

### Tier 2 — Medium-confidence market and platform evidence
Market pages, platform landing pages, reports, product descriptions, buyer guides and consulting-firm resources. These are useful for understanding positioning, business models, use cases and feature gaps, but they may be promotional.

### Tier 3 — Qualitative community evidence
Reddit, public forums, YouTube comments, Telegram/Facebook references and expat discussions are treated as qualitative signals only. They are useful for discovering repeated pains, language used by users, fears, confusion patterns and hidden frictions. They are not representative statistics unless sampled with a formal method.

## How the analysis was structured

The questions were converted into four research blocks:

1. **Pain discovery:** identify repeated relocation frictions across visas, tax, cost of living, housing, healthcare, banking, jobs, schools, documents, settlement and long-term citizenship.
2. **Competitor mapping:** group platforms by user job-to-be-done rather than by product category only.
3. **Feature gap analysis:** test each competitor against the same checklist: comparison, personalisation, AI/summaries, community, evidence, legal tracking, alerts and visual decision support.
4. **Monetisation analysis:** separate consumer willingness to pay from B2B willingness to pay, because the value logic is different.

## Important limitations

1. **Prices are unstable.** Some platforms show prices only after login, by country, by plan, or after sales contact. Prices in this package should be treated as current signals, not guaranteed quotes.
2. **Visa and tax information changes quickly.** A platform can be accurate today and wrong next month. This is one of the strongest product opportunities in the market.
3. **Crowdsourced cost-of-living data is useful but imperfect.** Numbeo, Expatistan and Nomads.com are helpful for initial orientation but can diverge from a real personal budget because rent, district, lifestyle, taxation, family size, healthcare and exchange rates matter.
4. **Community advice is context-specific.** Two contradictory answers can both be correct if they apply to different nationalities, dates, consulates, family situations, municipalities or tax-residency statuses.
5. **B2B relocation platforms are not direct substitutes for consumer country-choice tools.** Localyze, Jobbatical, Remote, Deel, Cartus, Graebel and Sirva solve corporate mobility and compliance problems; they prove monetisation potential but serve a different buyer.

## Practical verification rule

For any serious relocation decision, the user should verify four layers:

1. **Official rule:** government/embassy/tax authority source.
2. **Professional interpretation:** lawyer, tax adviser or regulated consultant for the user’s exact situation.
3. **Recent lived experience:** community reports from people with the same nationality/status/family profile.
4. **Date stamp:** when the rule or experience was last checked.

A future platform should make these four layers visible in the interface.
