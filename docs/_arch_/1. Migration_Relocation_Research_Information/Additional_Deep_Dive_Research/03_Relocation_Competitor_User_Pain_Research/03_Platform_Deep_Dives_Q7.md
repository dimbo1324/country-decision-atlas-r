# 7. Deep Analysis of Each Platform

## Executive answer

Most platforms are strong because they specialise. They are weak because relocation is interdisciplinary. The strongest product opportunity lies between categories: combine the **structured data discipline** of Numbeo/OECD, the **community signal** of InterNations/Expat.com/Nomads.com, the **workflow discipline** of Localyze/Jobbatical, the **legal tracking** of FiscalNote/LegiScan/State Net, and the **decision-support UX** of country/city comparison tools.

## 7.2 Numbeo

**Primary user:** individuals comparing costs, researchers, journalists, analysts and companies needing broad cost/QoL benchmarks.  
**Main problem solved:** “How expensive is this city/country compared with another?”  
**User result:** item-level price orientation, cost-of-living index, rent index, crime/safety/healthcare/traffic/quality-of-life indicators and paid API access.

### Why users choose it

- It has strong brand recognition.
- It covers many cities/countries.
- It is fast and free for basic research.
- It has API/data products for software use.

### Strongest functions

- city and country rankings;
- side-by-side comparison;
- item-level prices;
- quality-of-life, crime, healthcare, traffic and property-price sections;
- API access.

### Weakest functions

- crowdsourced data can be noisy;
- newcomer housing costs can differ from averages;
- weak visa/tax/legal context;
- weak personalisation beyond city comparison;
- no relocation workflow.

### Checklist

- Country/city comparison: yes.
- Personalisation: limited.
- AI/summaries: no major core AI layer visible.
- Community: contributors exist, but it is not a social platform.
- Source/evidence: methodology and data counts, but not claim-level official evidence.
- Legal tracking: no.
- Alerts: limited/not core.
- Dashboards: rankings and tables.

## 7.3 Expatistan

**Primary user:** people comparing two cities or countries quickly.  
**Main problem solved:** “Is city A cheaper than city B?”  
**User result:** fast cost comparison and index orientation.

### Strongest functions

- simple cost-of-living comparison;
- country/city index;
- item-level categories;
- easy UX.

### Weakest functions

- narrower scope than Numbeo;
- limited personalisation;
- no visa/legal/tax decision support;
- crowdsourced-data limitations.

### Checklist

- Comparison: yes, strong for cost.
- Personalisation: low.
- AI: no.
- Community: data contribution but no rich community.
- Evidence: limited.
- Legal tracking: no.
- Alerts: no.
- Visuals: simple tables/indexes.

## 7.4 Nomads.com

**Primary user:** digital nomads, remote workers and location-independent travellers.  
**Main problem solved:** “Where should I live/work remotely next, and where can I meet other nomads?”  
**User result:** city shortlist, cost estimate, internet/weather/safety filters, community access, travel tracking, meetups and visa/residence-related content.

### Why users choose it

- It is category-native for digital nomads.
- It combines data and community.
- It has practical filters: cost, internet, weather, safety, fun, air quality, women safety, LGBT friendliness and other lifestyle signals.
- It reduces loneliness through paid community and meetups.

### Strongest functions

- city rankings and filters;
- nomad cost estimates;
- community/chat/meetups;
- travel history and future plans;
- member-generated city signals;
- residence-calendar style tax-day awareness.

### Weakest functions

- selection bias: paid members are not representative of all migrants;
- cost data may not fit families, low-skilled migrants, students or retirees;
- legal content is not equivalent to legal advice;
- paid access can be high-friction for casual users;
- not a full PR/citizenship/tax/housing decision engine.

### Checklist

- Comparison: yes, strong for nomads.
- Personalisation: filters and profile/travel data.
- AI/summaries: limited/unclear as a core legal engine.
- Community: strong.
- Evidence: mixed; community plus platform data.
- Legal tracking: limited visa/residence information, not full legal tracker.
- Alerts: some personalised/travel-residence functionality.
- Visuals: strong cards/rankings.

## 7.5 InterNations

**Primary user:** expats who want community, events, local contacts and destination advice.  
**Main problem solved:** “How do I meet other expats and settle socially?”  
**User result:** events, groups, guides, forums, networking and Expat Insider rankings.

### Strongest functions

- expat community in many cities;
- events and groups;
- approved-member trust positioning;
- Expat Insider survey and rankings;
- destination guides.

### Weakest functions

- paid membership friction;
- not a legal/tax decision platform;
- survey samples may not represent all migrant types;
- not designed for deep country-choice modelling.

### Checklist

- Comparison: yes through Expat Insider rankings, not a full scenario engine.
- Personalisation: location/community based.
- AI: not central.
- Community: strong.
- Evidence: survey reports; limited official-source claim tracking.
- Legal tracking: no.
- Alerts: events/community updates.
- Visuals: reports/rankings.

## 7.6 Expat.com

**Primary user:** expats, migrants and people preparing a move.  
**Main problem solved:** “Where can I ask practical questions and read local expat experiences?”  
**User result:** forums, guides, Q&A, jobs, housing/service links and country-specific discussions.

### Strongest functions

- large community;
- country/city forums;
- guides;
- lived experience and Q&A.

### Weakest functions

- thread quality varies;
- outdated forum posts can mislead;
- not a structured decision engine;
- weak source verification.

### Checklist

- Comparison: medium through content, not structured.
- Personalisation: low.
- AI: no major visible core.
- Community: strong.
- Evidence: mostly user experience.
- Legal tracking: no.
- Alerts: forum updates.
- Visuals: basic.

## 7.7 Expat Arrivals

**Primary user:** corporate expats, families and people preparing destination-specific moves.  
**Main problem solved:** “Give me a practical country/city guide before relocation.”  
**User result:** curated destination guides on accommodation, visas, healthcare, schools, lifestyle and services.

### Strongest functions

- structured guides;
- local experts/contributors;
- family/corporate expat orientation;
- service referrals such as insurance.

### Weakest functions

- less interactive;
- not personalised;
- no full legal-change tracking;
- commercial partner bias possible.

## 7.8 International Living

**Primary user:** retirees, near-retirees, Americans/English-speaking users planning overseas lifestyle, property or retirement.  
**Main problem solved:** “Where can I retire or live better for less?”  
**User result:** country reports, retirement index, magazine, events, premium content and country narratives.

### Strongest functions

- compelling destination storytelling;
- retirement-specific framing;
- reports and memberships;
- country budgets and lifestyle descriptions;
- monetisation experience.

### Weakest functions

- promotional tone can be strong;
- less suitable for workers, students and complex legal cases;
- may understate hard bureaucracy or local integration challenges;
- must be cross-checked with official rules.

## 7.9 OECD Better Life Index

**Primary user:** policy-aware individuals, analysts, students, researchers and people comparing developed countries.  
**Main problem solved:** “Which countries perform better on the wellbeing dimensions I value?”  
**User result:** interactive country ranking based on user-weighted wellbeing indicators.

### Strongest functions

- official/institutional credibility;
- clear wellbeing dimensions;
- user weighting;
- clean visual comparison.

### Weakest functions

- no visa/tax/housing workflow;
- not city-level enough for relocation;
- limited coverage relative to global relocation needs;
- not designed for migrants with specific passports/income.

## 7.10 The Earth Awaits

**Primary user:** retirees, FIRE users, budget-conscious movers and long-stay travellers.  
**Main problem solved:** “Where can I live on my budget and lifestyle?”  
**User result:** city suggestions based on family size, accommodation needs, lifestyle level and budget.

### Strongest functions

- personal budget parameters;
- discovery experience;
- more useful than generic cost index for first shortlist;
- good for “where could I afford to live?”

### Weakest functions

- no deep visa/tax/citizenship logic;
- limited legal evidence;
- data freshness needs verification;
- not a full migration planner.

## 7.14 Henley & Partners

**Primary user:** high-net-worth individuals, family offices, advisers and governments.  
**Main problem solved:** “How do I obtain alternative residence/citizenship through investment and strategic planning?”  
**User result:** advisory, program comparison, reports, indices and bespoke planning.

### Strongest functions

- brand authority;
- long institutional track record;
- program index methodology;
- global advisory network;
- strong HNWI trust.

### Weakest functions

- expensive/high-end focus;
- not designed for ordinary workers/students/nomads;
- less community and daily-life detail;
- consultation-driven rather than self-serve decision engine.

## 7.15 FiscalNote / PolicyNote

**Primary user:** public affairs teams, companies, NGOs, legal/compliance teams and government-relations professionals.  
**Main problem solved:** “How do we track relevant laws, bills, regulations and policy risks?”  
**User result:** AI-powered policy monitoring, alerts, summaries, issue management and analysis.

### Strongest functions

- AI policy summaries;
- alerts and curation;
- legislative/regulatory monitoring;
- workflow for teams;
- global policy-intelligence positioning.

### Weakest functions

- B2B cost and complexity;
- not relocation-specific;
- not a consumer country-choice UX;
- user still needs domain translation from law to relocation consequence.

### Product lesson

A relocation platform can borrow the legal-tracking architecture but translate legal events into migrant consequences.

## 7.22 Cross-platform pattern analysis

### What competitors do well

- Cost tools are fast and broad.
- Community tools reveal real-life problems.
- Digital nomad tools understand remote-worker lifestyle.
- Investment-migration firms understand long-term status strategy.
- Corporate relocation platforms understand workflow and case management.
- Legal-intelligence platforms understand law tracking and alerts.

### What competitors do poorly

- They rarely connect legal eligibility with daily-life feasibility.
- They rarely show source confidence at claim level.
- They rarely personalise by passport, income, family, job, language and long-term goal.
- They rarely track changes over time in a user-friendly way.
- They rarely integrate community stories into structured data.
- They rarely explain contradictions.
- They rarely distinguish tourist, nomad, student, worker, investor, retiree and family needs.

### Most important gap

The market lacks a **relocation decision operating system**: a platform that helps a user move from vague interest to verified shortlist, then to action plan, then to post-arrival compliance and long-term status tracking.
