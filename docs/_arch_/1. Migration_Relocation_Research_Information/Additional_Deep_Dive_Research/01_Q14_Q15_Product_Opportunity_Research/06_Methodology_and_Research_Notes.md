# Methodology and Research Notes

**Research date:** 2026-06-17

## Approach

This package combines:

1. The uploaded archive as a formatting and topic-coverage reference.
2. Current public sources checked during preparation.
3. Competitor/product analysis.
4. Strategic product-design reasoning.

## Source hierarchy

### Level A — official sources
Examples:
- government immigration portals;
- official EU Commission material;
- official national ministry pages.

Used for legal and route facts.

### Level B — institutional sources
Examples:
- UN DESA;
- IOM;
- UNHCR;
- OECD;
- World Bank;
- V-Dem.

Used for macro trends, governance, migration flows, country indicators.

### Level C — professional/expert sources
Examples:
- Fragomen;
- Mercer;
- EY;
- IBA;
- recognised law/accounting firms.

Used for trend interpretation and specialised mobility insight.

### Level D — platforms/databases
Examples:
- Numbeo;
- Expatistan;
- Nomads.com;
- InterNations;
- Expat.com;
- VisaGuide.World;
- Henley Passport Index.

Used for competitor analysis and data-layer comparison.

### Level E — community signals
Examples:
- Reddit;
- Telegram;
- YouTube;
- Facebook groups;
- forums.

Used only as qualitative evidence of repeated user pains, not as statistical truth.

## What this package does not do

- It does not provide legal advice.
- It does not rank countries as universally “best”.
- It does not claim that any visa route is guaranteed.
- It does not validate every country-specific legal rule.
- It does not perform full tax analysis.

## Confidence labelling

- **High confidence:** repeated across source types or based on official/institutional data.
- **Medium confidence:** based on credible but not exhaustive sources.
- **Low confidence:** qualitative signal, anecdotal, or requires further research.
