# 15. Final Check Before Product Design

## Q15.1. Which 10–15 countries should be analysed first and why?

A first-country set should not be chosen only by popularity. It should combine:
- high migration demand;
- policy volatility;
- data availability;
- route diversity;
- business/investment relevance;
- digital-nomad relevance;
- user-community volume;
- regional diversity;
- high decision risk.

## Recommended 15-country set

| Priority | Country | Why it should be included early | Main user scenarios |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Germany | Major skilled-worker destination; official reforms around skilled migration and Opportunity Card; strong labour-market need; rich official data. | Skilled workers, students, families |
| 2 | Spain | Strong digital-nomad and lifestyle demand; EU access; tax/legal complexity; high community interest. | Digital nomads, families, founders |
| 3 | Portugal | Very popular relocation destination; recent immigration/nationality/family-reunification changes; high gap between old online content and current reality. | Remote workers, families, investors |
| 4 | Canada | Large migration market; official target changes; tension between openness and housing/temporary-resident reduction. | Skilled workers, students, families |
| 5 | United Kingdom | High demand, but rules tightened; salary/skill thresholds and settlement changes create decision risk. | Skilled workers, students, founders |
| 6 | United States | Largest aspirational destination; complex route structure; high demand but high uncertainty and polarised policy. | Skilled workers, students, founders, investors |
| 7 | Netherlands | High-quality-life destination with housing pressure; useful for EU skilled migration, tech workers and expats. | Skilled workers, families |
| 8 | United Arab Emirates | Strong business, tax and remote-work appeal; residence routes, company formation and banking matter. | Entrepreneurs, investors, remote workers |
| 9 | Singapore | High-income, high-governance business hub; difficult access; strong benchmark for premium migration. | Skilled workers, founders, investors |
| 10 | Mexico | Very popular with remote workers and North Americans; cost/lifestyle appeal; tax/residence reality often misunderstood. | Digital nomads, retirees, families |
| 11 | Thailand | Major nomad/lifestyle destination; new/updated longer-stay route interest; gap between lifestyle content and legal/tax reality. | Digital nomads, remote workers |
| 12 | Japan | Newer digital-nomad route, strict constraints; strong contrast between lifestyle appeal and residence limitations. | Digital nomads, skilled workers |
| 13 | South Korea | Growing tech/culture appeal; digital-nomad/startup interest; language and employment barriers. | Digital nomads, students, tech workers |
| 14 | Uruguay | Stable small-country option in Latin America; relevant for long-term residence, safety, citizenship and regional comparison. | Residence seekers, remote workers, families |
| 15 | Saudi Arabia | Rapid transformation, business/talent attraction, high policy interest, but major cultural/legal fit questions. | Business, skilled workers, investors |

## Q15.2. Which 3–5 user scenarios are most promising?

## Scenario 1 — Skilled worker choosing a country

### User question
“Where can I realistically move for work, legal residence and career growth?”

### Why promising
- high willingness to pay;
- strong demand from employers and migrants;
- many legal routes;
- objective criteria available;
- repeat use as rules change.

### Required data
- eligible occupations;
- salary thresholds;
- job-market demand;
- recognition of qualifications;
- language requirements;
- work visa timeline;
- family rights;
- path to PR/citizenship.

### MVP value
Personalised shortlist + route comparison + document checklist.

---

## Scenario 2 — Digital nomad / remote earner

### User question
“Where can I legally live while working remotely, without tax or visa surprises?”

### Why promising
- fast-growing category;
- many countries market nomad visas;
- high confusion about legality and tax;
- strong content/community demand.

### Required data
- remote-work visa availability;
- income threshold;
- stay duration;
- renewal;
- tax residency;
- dependants;
- health insurance;
- local-work restrictions;
- banking and housing.

### MVP value
Legal-base comparison + tax-risk warning + lifestyle/cost comparison.

---

## Scenario 3 — Founder / entrepreneur / small business owner

### User question
“Where can I relocate, register a business, hire people, pay taxes reasonably and live legally?”

### Why promising
- higher willingness to pay;
- overlap with legal, tax and accounting services;
- B2B potential;
- high pain due to fragmented information.

### Required data
- company formation;
- founder visas;
- startup visas;
- corporate tax;
- personal tax;
- banking;
- hiring rules;
- compliance;
- residence route;
- investor/founder PR path.

### MVP value
Business relocation map + red flags + expert referral.

---

## Scenario 4 — Student / early-career migrant

### User question
“Where should I study if I want to work and stay after graduation?”

### Why promising
- large global market;
- clear pathway logic;
- strong long-term value;
- easy to monetise through guides, university partners, consultations.

### Required data
- student visa;
- tuition;
- work rights during study;
- post-study work permit;
- skill-shortage lists;
- language requirements;
- graduate employment;
- PR/citizenship path.

### MVP value
Study-to-stay country comparison.

---

## Scenario 5 — Family / long-term residence seeker

### User question
“Where can my family live safely, legally and affordably, with healthcare, school and future citizenship?”

### Why promising
- emotionally strong problem;
- high decision risk;
- high willingness to pay for certainty;
- very poorly solved by generic rankings.

### Required data
- family reunification;
- spouse work rights;
- school access;
- childcare;
- healthcare;
- rental market;
- neighbourhood/city safety;
- residence stability;
- citizenship timeline.

### MVP value
Family suitability score + hidden-risk checklist.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q15.3. Which data is mandatory for the first version?

The MVP should focus on data that directly reduces decision risk.

## Mandatory MVP data

### 1. Country identity data
- country name;
- region;
- currency;
- main cities;
- official language(s);
- basic population/economy indicators.

### 2. Route data
For each country:
- main visa/residence routes;
- who each route is for;
- eligibility;
- required income/salary;
- required documents;
- stay duration;
- renewal;
- work rights;
- family rights;
- path to PR;
- path to citizenship;
- official source link;
- last checked date.

### 3. Cost baseline
- single-person monthly estimate;
- family monthly estimate;
- rent range;
- deposit expectation;
- utilities;
- healthcare/insurance;
- transport;
- initial relocation buffer.

### 4. Legal-change data
- recent immigration/legal changes;
- effective date;
- affected route;
- before/after rule;
- user impact;
- source;
- confidence.

### 5. Quality-of-life indicators
- safety;
- healthcare access;
- education/family suitability;
- internet;
- climate;
- language barrier;
- bureaucracy friction.

### 6. Work/business data
- labour-market attractiveness;
- common shortage sectors;
- employer sponsorship difficulty;
- business setup complexity;
- tax headline.

### 7. Source metadata
Every important claim must have:
- source URL;
- source type;
- publication/update date;
- last checked date;
- confidence level;
- owner/editor.

### 8. Scoring fields
- user scenario;
- criteria weights;
- data completeness;
- confidence;
- warning flags.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q15.5. Which functions give immediate value?

### Immediate-value MVP functions

1. **Country cards with trustworthy summaries**
   - user quickly understands a country.

2. **Side-by-side country comparison**
   - user can compare 2–4 countries.

3. **Persona-based fit score**
   - country result changes based on user type.

4. **Route explorer**
   - user sees realistic legal routes.

5. **Recent law changes**
   - user sees what changed and why it matters.

6. **Source-backed answers**
   - every important claim has a source.

7. **Risk flags**
   - “This route does not lead to citizenship.”
   - “Family rights are limited.”
   - “Cost data is low-confidence.”
   - “Law changed recently.”
   - “Tax advice required.”

8. **First-step checklist**
   - user knows what to do next.

9. **Scenario simulator**
   - user adjusts income, family status, timeline, goal.

10. **Saved shortlist**
   - user can build a country decision list.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q15.6. Which functions are too complex for MVP?

### Too complex for first release

1. **Full legal interpretation for 200 countries**
   - too risky;
   - too much manual review;
   - high liability.

2. **Accurate visa approval probability**
   - requires proprietary data;
   - varies by consulate, nationality, case quality.

3. **Full tax calculator**
   - tax is complex and high-risk;
   - requires expert review.

4. **Real-time law tracking everywhere**
   - legal gazettes are fragmented;
   - language/local interpretation needed.

5. **Verified global user-story network**
   - moderation and verification are heavy.

6. **Live housing availability**
   - scraping/API/licensing complexity;
   - market moves quickly.

7. **Lawyer marketplace**
   - trust, compliance, liability and quality control.

8. **Automatic document preparation**
   - legal risk and country-specific complexity.

9. **Full AI legal assistant**
   - hallucination and liability risk.

10. **Country prediction engine**
   - forecasting policy is difficult; start with transparent trend indicators.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q15.7. Which functions help differentiate from competitors?

### Strong differentiators

1. **Country Direction Index**
   - shows movement over time, not only current ranking.

2. **Law Impact Tracker**
   - explains new laws by user persona.

3. **Evidence Map**
   - separates official, expert, institutional and community sources.

4. **Source freshness labels**
   - reduces outdated-information risk.

5. **Personalised scenario score**
   - user sees country fit for their exact case.

6. **Contradiction warning**
   - platform detects when sources disagree.

7. **Real case + official rule pairing**
   - user sees both law and practice.

8. **Bad-decision prevention**
   - platform explicitly warns against common mistakes.

9. **Transparent methodology**
   - every score can be opened and audited.

10. **Manual/AI hybrid editorial model**
   - faster than a law firm, safer than a pure chatbot.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q15.8. Which functions can realistically be implemented without a large team?

### Realistic small-team MVP

1. **Static-but-structured country database**
   - 10–15 countries;
   - JSON/DB schema;
   - manually curated sources.

2. **Country cards**
   - generated from structured fields.

   - transparent weights;

5. **AI-assisted summaries**
   - summarise official sources and editor notes;

6. **Legal-change feed**
   - manually curated first;

7. **Source index**
   - source type;
   - last checked;
   - confidence.

8. **Basic user questionnaire**
   - profile;
   - goals;
   - constraints;
   - risk tolerance.

9. **Scenario result page**
   - best countries;
   - why;
   - risks;
   - next steps.

10. **Admin/editor panel**
   - create/update country data;
   - mark source status;
   - approve AI summaries.

### Recommended team for MVP

- 1 full-stack developer;
- 1 research/editorial owner;

## Q15.10. Where can automatic data collection be used?

### Good candidates for automation

1. **Institutional indicators**
   - World Bank;
   - OECD;
   - UN;
   - UNHCR;
   - IOM;
   - V-Dem;
   - Eurostat.

2. **Source freshness monitoring**
   - check if a source URL changed;
   - detect publication dates;
   - flag stale pages.

3. **News/legal alert ingestion**
   - RSS feeds;
   - government press releases;
   - law-firm newsletters;
   - official gazette monitoring where accessible.

4. **Cost data via licensed API or controlled source**
   - do not scrape aggressively without permission;
   - store date and source.

5. **Basic country facts**
   - currency;
   - language;
   - population;
   - GDP;
   - unemployment;
   - inflation.

6. **Search-trend signals**
   - Google Trends-style interest;
   - YouTube/Reddit topic volume where APIs/terms permit.

7. **Community topic clustering**
   - repeated pains;
   - country-specific complaints;
   - common questions.

8. **Broken-link detection**
   - essential for trust.

### Important caution

Automatic collection should not automatically become legal advice. It should feed an editorial queue.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q15.11. Where can AI be used?

### Best AI use cases

1. **Source summarisation**
   - convert long government pages into plain-language summaries.

2. **Before/after legal comparison**
   - extract old rule vs new rule from editor-approved sources.

3. **Visa requirement extraction**
   - income threshold;
   - documents;
   - duration;
   - renewal;
   - dependants.

4. **Persona impact draft**
   - “What does this change mean for digital nomads?”
   - “What does this change mean for families?”

5. **Contradiction detection**
   - compare official source, old blog, lawyer note, user story.

6. **Community signal mining**
   - cluster repeated pains;
   - detect common mistakes;
   - extract frequently asked questions.

7. **Country summary generation**
   - draft country card;
   - generate pros/cons;
   - produce user-specific explanation.

8. **Scenario explanation**
   - explain why one country scored higher than another.

9. **Checklist generation**
   - generate first-step checklist from route data.

10. **Translation/localisation**
   - translate official/source material into English and other languages, with editor review.

### AI should not do alone

- final legal advice;
- final tax advice;
- final visa eligibility decision;
- final investment recommendation;
- final healthcare/school recommendation;
- final safety claim.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q15.12. Where is human verification mandatory?

### Human verification is mandatory for

1. **All legal/visa route claims**
   - especially if shown as recommendation.

2. **Tax-residency and tax-liability warnings**
   - high financial risk.

3. **Citizenship timelines**
   - high user expectations and frequent law changes.

4. **Family reunification**
   - emotional and legal sensitivity.

5. **Healthcare access**
   - serious real-world consequences.

6. **Investment migration**
   - fraud, sanctions, source-of-funds and regulatory risk.

7. **Asylum/humanitarian topics**
   - extremely sensitive and high-stakes.

8. **User story verification**
   - fake, outdated or misleading stories can harm users.

9. **Country-direction labels**
   - “authoritarian”, “open”, “restrictive”, “business-friendly” must be source-backed and carefully defined.

10. **Public scoring methodology**
   - transparent and reviewed to avoid misleading users.

### Recommended verification workflow

1. AI drafts extraction.
2. Researcher checks source.
3. Editor checks clarity and bias.
4. Lawyer/tax expert checks high-risk fields.
5. Publish with confidence label.
6. Schedule next review.
7. Re-check after law/news changes.

**Confidence:** high.
