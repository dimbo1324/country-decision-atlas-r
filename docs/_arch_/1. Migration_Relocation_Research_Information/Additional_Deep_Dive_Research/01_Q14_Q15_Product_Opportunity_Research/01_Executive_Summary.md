# Executive Summary — Q14–Q15

**Research date:** 2026-06-17

## Main conclusion

The strongest opportunity is not “another country ranking website”. The market already has many fragments:

- **cost data** from Numbeo, Expatistan, Mercer and similar tools;
- **city/nomad rankings** from Nomads.com and digital-nomad guides;
- **visa summaries** from VisaGuide.World, law firms, relocation agencies and government portals;
- **expat stories and subjective reviews** from Expat.com, InterNations, Reddit, Facebook groups, Telegram, YouTube and blogs;
- **institutional indicators** from OECD, World Bank, UN, IOM, UNHCR, V-Dem and similar sources.

The gap is that these sources rarely become a **decision system**. They give information, but they usually do not answer:

> “Given my passport, income, profession, family situation, risk tolerance, preferred lifestyle, tax exposure and long-term citizenship goal, which country is realistically suitable — and what has changed recently that could make my decision wrong?”

## Product opportunity in one sentence

Build a **Country Decision OS**: a platform that combines official data, legal-change tracking, country trajectory, cost-of-life reality, user stories, expert review, AI summaries and scenario simulation into one transparent decision-support system.

## Key unclosed opportunities

1. **Evidence-backed country cards**  
   Every score and claim should have source links, source type, last checked date and confidence level.

2. **Personalised country-fit scoring**  
   The user should not see a generic ranking only. They should see country fit by profile: skilled worker, digital nomad, student, founder, investor, family, retiree, safety-seeker.

3. **Law-change impact layer**  
   Existing platforms often publish visa pages, but they do not clearly show how a new law affects migrants, business owners, remote workers, families or investors.

4. **Country trajectory over time**  
   Most platforms show static snapshots. A stronger product shows whether a country is becoming more open/restrictive, expensive/affordable, stable/unstable, business-friendly/bureaucratic, democratic/authoritarian, migrant-friendly/hostile.

5. **Verified real stories connected to data**  
   Reviews alone are subjective. Official data alone is dry. The opportunity is to join both: real stories tagged by passport, route, city, budget, family status, job field and outcome.

6. **Contradiction detection**  
   Users often face conflicting advice. A new platform can show: official rule, lawyer interpretation, community experience, last update, contradiction warning, and what still needs human verification.

7. **MVP that is realistic for a small team**  
   Start with 10–15 countries, 4–5 user scenarios, a controlled data schema, curated official sources, AI-assisted summaries and human review for legal/tax-sensitive fields.

A smaller MVP can start with **Germany, Spain, Portugal, Canada, UK, UAE, Mexico, Thailand, Japan and Uruguay**.

## Strongest first user scenarios

1. Skilled worker choosing a country for work/residence.
2. Digital nomad or remote earner choosing a legal base.
3. Founder / small entrepreneur choosing a business-friendly jurisdiction.
4. Student or early-career migrant choosing study-to-work pathway.
5. Family / long-term residence seeker choosing stability, healthcare, schools and citizenship path.

## What must be in the MVP

- Country card.
- Country comparison.
- Visa/residence route overview.
- Source-backed “last checked” fields.
- Cost-of-living baseline.
- Legal-change feed.
- Simple user-profile questionnaire.
- Fit score with visible methodology.
- Scenario simulator.
- Risk warnings.
- Manual editorial review for sensitive claims.

## What should wait

- Full automated legal interpretation for every country.
- Tax residency calculator.
- Real visa-probability prediction.
- Full job-market matching.
- Real-time housing availability.
- Fully verified global community.
- Lawyer marketplace.
- Document automation.

## Strategic positioning

The platform should position itself as:

> “A transparent, evidence-based relocation intelligence platform that helps people compare countries, understand legal changes, and make safer migration, residence, work, business and citizenship decisions.”

It should not position itself as only a visa blog, travel ranking, expat forum, cost calculator or AI chatbot.
