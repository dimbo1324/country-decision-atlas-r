# 9. Monetisation and Willingness to Pay — Questions 91–100

## Q91. What relocation information are people willing to pay for?

People pay for information that reduces legal, financial or life-changing risk:
- visa eligibility assessment;
- document checklist personalised to passport/family/income;
- residence/citizenship route comparison;
- tax-residency analysis;
- country-specific relocation guides;
- school/neighbourhood guidance;
- trusted lawyer/agent referrals;
- processing timeline/backlog intelligence;
- investment migration comparison;
- business setup and banking guidance;
- relocation budget calculators;
- family-specific planning.

Generic blog content is hard to monetise. Personalised, verified and time-sensitive guidance is monetisable.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q93. How much do migration, visa, residence, PR and citizenship consultations cost?

Pricing varies by country and provider. Typical online first consultations can range from about USD/EUR 50–300 for basic immigration advice. Specialist lawyers in high-cost countries may charge USD/EUR 200–600+ per hour. Full visa package support can range from several hundred to several thousand dollars/euros. Investment migration and complex tax planning can cost much more, often with free initial calls but high implementation fees.

Example: a German immigration-law firm publicly lists short first consultations around €109 and comprehensive consultations around €226.10.

**Confidence:** medium-high.

## Q94. How much do paid reports, guides and checklists cost?

Consumer relocation guides often range from free to USD/EUR 20–150. Premium digital nomad memberships may be around USD 99 for full access, with lower tiers or promotions. Lawyer-produced country guides may be free as lead magnets but monetise consultation. High-net-worth investment migration reports may be free marketing material, while personalised jurisdiction reports or tax memoranda can cost hundreds or thousands.

**Confidence:** medium.

## Q95. Which services use a subscription model?

Examples:
- Nomads.com/Nomad List membership;
- InterNations premium community features;
- some relocation/cost-of-living platforms;
- FiscalNote, LexisNexis State Net, Quorum and other law-tracking tools;
- B2B global mobility SaaS platforms;
- HR/payroll/global employment platforms such as Deel/Remote-like systems;
- country intelligence/data platforms.

Subscription works best when data changes continuously or community/network access is valuable.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q96. Which services earn money from legal consultations?

Immigration law firms, global mobility firms and investment migration consultancies monetize consultations and case management. Examples include Fragomen, Newland Chase, local immigration lawyers, IAS/Total Law-style firms, Henley & Partners, Astons, CS Global Partners and many country-specific law firms. Some sites provide free SEO guides and route users into paid consultation funnels.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q97. Which services earn through affiliate programmes?

Relocation platforms and blogs often monetise:
- insurance;
- international money transfers;
- banks/fintech;
- temporary housing;
- moving/shipping;
- visa services;
- language schools;
- coworking;
- tax consultations;
- company formation;
- travel bookings;
- eSIM/mobile plans.

Affiliate incentives can bias recommendations, so transparent disclosure is important.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q98. What B2B services exist in global mobility and relocation?

B2B services include:

- tax equalisation and assignment cost tools;
- business travel compliance;
- remote work approval systems;

**Confidence:** high.

## Q99. What do companies pay for in employee relocation?

Companies pay for:
- visa/work permit processing;
- immigration compliance;
- payroll/tax/social security compliance;
- relocation allowance;
- flights;
- temporary accommodation;
- household goods shipment;
- home search;
- school search;
- spouse support;
- language/cultural training;
- assignment compensation data;
- risk management;
- HR workflow tracking;
- reporting and cost control.

The core B2B value is not “moving boxes”; it is reducing compliance risk and retaining talent.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q100. Which paid functions do users consider truly valuable?

Valuable paid functions:
- personalised eligibility check;
- reliable document checklist;
- official-source-linked visa explanation;
- lawyer-reviewed route comparison;
- realistic budget including rent/tax/healthcare;
- country/city comparison by personal priorities;
- alerts when rules change;
- human review before application;
- verified community experience by profile;
- settlement checklist;
- tax-residency warning system;
- employer sponsorship/job pathway database.

Users are less willing to pay for generic rankings, obvious blog posts or unverified “best country” lists.

**Confidence:** high.
