# 8. Existing Services and Platforms — Questions 79–90

## Q79. What sites and apps do people use to choose a country?

People use a fragmented stack:
- Numbeo and Expatistan for cost of living;
- Nomads.com/Nomad List for digital-nomad city comparisons;
- Expat.com and InterNations for expat guides/community;
- Reddit/Facebook/Telegram for lived experience;
- government immigration portals for visas;
- OECD/World Bank/UN data for macro indicators;
- Mercer/OECD Better Life/World Happiness/Global Peace Index for quality-of-life signals;
- relocation firms and immigration-lawyer sites for practical pathways;
- YouTube for narrative “what it’s really like” content.

No single platform fully solves country choice across law, data, community and personal fit.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q80. Which sites compare cost of living across countries?

Common tools:
- Numbeo;
- Expatistan;
- Nomads.com/Nomad List;
- Mercer Cost of Living for corporate mobility;
- Wise/Remitly blogs and calculators;
- local salary/rent portals;
- government statistics offices;
- newer tools such as WhereNext or affordability calculators that combine taxes and rent.

Crowdsourced tools are useful for rough comparison but can be inaccurate for current rents and expat-specific spending.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q81. Which sites compare quality of life?

Useful sources:
- OECD Better Life Index;
- World Happiness Report;
- Mercer Quality of Living Ranking;
- Global Peace Index / Vision of Humanity;
- Numbeo Quality of Life Index;
- Teleport-style city rankings;
- InterNations Expat Insider surveys;
- government statistics portals;
- World Bank governance indicators.

Quality of life should be decomposed into safety, health, income, housing, education, environment, social trust and personal rights.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q82. Which sites help find visa and migration rules?

Best sources:
- official government immigration portals;
- official consulate websites;
- EU Immigration Portal;
- Make it in Germany;
- Canadian IRCC, Australian Home Affairs, UK GOV.UK, New Zealand Immigration, US USCIS/State Department;
- KPMG/EY/Fragomen/Newland Chase immigration updates;
- immigration lawyer explainers;
- Nomads.com or digital-nomad trackers for first-pass discovery.

Final decisions should never rely only on blogs.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q83. Which sites help with taxes and business abroad?

Useful sources:
- national tax authorities;
- Big Four tax/immigration guides: KPMG, EY, PwC, Deloitte;
- OECD tax resources;
- World Bank Doing Business legacy data and governance indicators;
- country-specific accounting firms;
- Stripe Atlas-type business formation resources;
- Deel/Remote global employment guides;
- legal/tax firms specializing in expats and entrepreneurs.

Tax content is often oversimplified online; expert advice is usually needed for business owners.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q84. Which sites help find reviews from migrants and expats?

Useful sources:
- Reddit;
- Expat.com;
- InterNations;
- Facebook expat groups;
- YouTube channels and comments;
- Telegram/WhatsApp groups;
- Trustpilot/Google reviews for lawyers and relocation agencies;
- country-specific forums;
- relocation blogs.

The problem is verification: reviews may be anecdotal, outdated, emotional or passport-specific.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q85. Which sites help digital nomads choose countries and cities?

Common tools:
- Nomads.com/Nomad List;
- Remote Year / digital nomad blogs;
- Citizen Remote-style visa directories;
- KPMG/EY remote work visa trackers;
- Reddit r/digitalnomad;
- coworking/city community groups;
- Airbnb/Booking for real rent signals;
- Speedtest/Ookla for internet;
- SafetyWing/Genki/insurance providers for health coverage;
- Google Maps and local community chats.

Nomad tools often underweight legal/tax details.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q86. Which sites help companies and HR departments relocate employees?

B2B/global mobility platforms include:
- Localyze;
- Deel;
- Remote;
- Topia;
- Work From Anywhere;
- Cartus, Graebel, Sirva, Crown, TRC Global Mobility;
- Fragomen and Newland Chase for immigration services;
- Mercer and AIRINC for cost-of-living and assignment compensation data.

Companies pay for compliance, immigration, tax, relocation logistics, policy management and employee experience.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q87. Which sites help track laws and political changes?

Relevant tools:
- FiscalNote/PolicyNote;
- LexisNexis State Net;
- LegiScan;
- Quorum;
- VLex/Lexis/Westlaw;
- government gazettes;
- parliamentary trackers;
- EU Law / EUR-Lex;
- World Bank WGI;
- IOM Migration Governance Indicators;
- Freedom House, V-Dem and Economist Intelligence Unit for political indicators.

Most law-tracking tools are B2B and expensive.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q88. Which services are most popular in migration, relocation and country choice?

Consumer/popular:
- Numbeo;
- Expatistan;
- Nomads.com/Nomad List;
- InterNations;
- Expat.com;
- Reddit/Facebook/Telegram communities;
- YouTube relocation channels;
- government portals;
- immigration-lawyer sites.

Professional/B2B:
- Fragomen;
- Newland Chase;
- Localyze;
- Deel;
- Remote;
- Topia;
- Mercer;
- Cartus/Graebel/Sirva/Crown;
- Henley & Partners/Astons/CS Global Partners for investment migration.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q89. Which functions of these services are most useful?

Most useful functions:
- city/country comparison;
- cost-of-living data;
- visa requirement summaries;
- community Q&A;
- relocation checklists;
- local guides;
- country ranking filters;
- tax/residence explainers;
- corporate workflow tracking;
- document management;
- employee immigration status tracking;
- alerts on legal changes;
- lawyer/consultant matching;
- neighbourhood and housing guidance.

The best functions reduce uncertainty and save verification time.

**Confidence:** high.

## Q90. Which functions are weakest or inconvenient?

Common weaknesses:
- outdated visa data;
- weak source traceability;
- crowd-sourced costs that do not match real rent;
- no personalisation by passport/income/family/job;
- no legal/tax integration;
- too much SEO content;
- hidden affiliate incentives;
- poor distinction between official law and anecdote;
- no timeline/backlog data;
- no risk score;
- paywalls before value is proven;
- community negativity/noise;
- B2B tools unsuitable for individual migrants.

**Confidence:** high.
