# 9. Monetisation and Willingness to Pay — Questions 91–100

## Q91. What relocation information are people willing to pay for?

People pay for information that reduces risk, saves time or prevents expensive mistakes:

- personalised visa route analysis;
- document checklist;
- tax-residence analysis;
- country comparison for their profile;
- school/healthcare/housing reports;
- relocation budget plan;
- legal updates;
- “what to do first” step-by-step plan;
- verified expert answers;
- scam/lawyer verification;
- second passport/residence strategy.

Generic blog content has low willingness to pay; personalised, verified, time-sensitive advice has high willingness to pay.

- tax consultations;
- company formation;

- school search;

- digital nomad visa support.

## Q93. How much do consultations on migration, visas, residence and citizenship cost?

Prices vary widely:

- basic immigration consultation: often USD/EUR 50–300;
- specialist lawyer consultation in developed markets: USD/EUR 200–700+ per hour;
- full visa support: hundreds to several thousand dollars/euros;
- business/investor immigration: several thousand to tens of thousands;
- citizenship-by-investment legal/service fees: often tens of thousands on top of government/investment amounts.

Exact country-specific prices require live local market research.

## Q94. How much do paid reports, guides and checklists cost?

Common ranges:

- simple PDF checklist: USD 5–30;
- detailed country guide: USD 20–100;
- personalised relocation report: USD 100–500;
- premium expert report with tax/legal notes: USD 500–2,000+;
- subscription country database: USD 10–50/month for consumers, much higher for B2B.

The perceived value increases when the guide is current, specific and actionable.

## Q95. Which services use subscription models?

Examples or categories:

- Nomads.com / Nomad List-style memberships;
- premium expat communities;
- legal update platforms;
- global mobility SaaS platforms;
- HR relocation dashboards;
- tax/compliance monitoring tools;
- country intelligence platforms;
- paid Telegram/Discord communities.

Consumer subscriptions work when content is updated frequently and community/network value exists.

## Q96. Which services earn from legal consultations?

Immigration law firms, relocation agencies, investment migration firms, Big Four global mobility practices, country-specific visa consultants and some content platforms monetize through consultations.

Typical funnel: free guide → eligibility quiz → paid consultation → full-service package.

## Q99. What do companies pay for in employee relocation?

Companies pay for:

- immigration compliance;
- visa sponsorship;
- legal filings;
- relocation flights;
- temporary housing;
- moving household goods;
- destination orientation;
- school search;
- tax support;
- payroll compliance;
- spouse support;
- cultural training;
- tracking and reporting;
- risk reduction.

Corporate relocation packages can be expensive, so B2B buyers value predictability, compliance and employee retention.

## Q100. Which paid features do users consider truly valuable?

Valuable paid features:

- personalised legal route map;
- verified official-source updates;
- document checklist by nationality and destination;
- cost-of-living calculator using personal lifestyle assumptions;
- tax-residence risk warning;
- lawyer/expert matching;
- community answers with verification layer;
- relocation timeline;
- housing and school intelligence;
- “country fit score” with explainable criteria;
- alerts when rules change.

Users pay when the product reduces fear and uncertainty, not merely when it displays information.
