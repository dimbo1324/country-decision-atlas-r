# 8. Existing Services and Platforms — Questions 79–90

## Q79. What websites and apps do people use to choose a country?

Common tools:

- Nomads.com / Nomad List;
- Numbeo;
- InterNations Expat Insider;
- Expatistan;
- Mercer cost-of-living data;
- OECD Better Life Index;
- World Bank Data;
- UN Human Development Index;
- Henley Passport Index;
- Global Citizen Solutions reports;
- country-specific immigration blogs;
- Reddit, Facebook groups, Telegram channels;
- YouTube relocation channels.

No single tool fully combines law, cost, quality of life, political direction, user experience and personal fit.

## Q80. Which sites compare cost of living?

Main tools:

- Numbeo;
- Expatistan;
- Mercer cost-of-living rankings;
- SalaryExpert / ERI tools;
- Nomads.com / Nomad List;
- Wise and local bank guides;
- city-specific rental platforms;
- Reddit community budgets.

Numbeo is popular and broad, but because it uses crowdsourced data, it must be treated as directional, not exact.

## Q81. Which sites compare quality of life?

Useful sources:

- InterNations Expat Insider;
- OECD Better Life Index;
- UN Human Development Index;
- World Happiness Report;
- Numbeo Quality of Life index;
- Mercer Quality of Living rankings;
- Economist Intelligence Unit liveability rankings;
- Social Progress Index;
- country-level healthcare/education/safety indices.

Different indices optimise for different users. A high GDP country may still be poor fit for a migrant because of housing or integration.

## Q83. Which sites help with taxes and business abroad?

- OECD tax publications;
- national tax authority websites;
- Nomad Capitalist / Offshore Citizen style content for planning ideas;

- local accounting firms;

Tax is one of the highest willingness-to-pay categories because mistakes are expensive.

## Q87. Which sites track laws and political changes?

- official gazettes and parliament websites;
- government consultation portals;
- EU EUR-Lex and EU Commission portals;
- OECD policy reports;
- EMN migration reports;
- Fragomen/KPMG/EY alerts;

## Q90. Which functions are weakest or inconvenient?

Common weaknesses:

- outdated legal information;
- no clear source date;
- weak personalisation;
- too much generic content;
- no distinction between tourist and resident life;
- poor legal/tax precision;
- weak community verification;
- limited non-English coverage;
- insufficient city/neighbourhood granularity;
- unclear methodology for rankings;
- no integration of policy direction and future risk.

The gap is not “another country ranking.” The gap is a **decision-support system with verified law, real-life evidence, current data, user persona weighting and change tracking**.
