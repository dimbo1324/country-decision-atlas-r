# 9. Monetisation and Willingness to Pay

## 91. What relocation information are people willing to pay for?

People pay for information that reduces legal, financial or life-changing uncertainty:

- visa eligibility;
- document checklist;
- legal pathway comparison;
- tax-residence analysis;
- cost-of-living budget;
- school/healthcare guidance;
- housing/neighbourhood intelligence;
- second citizenship/residence strategy;
- business setup;
- relocation timeline;
- personalised country shortlist.

They are less willing to pay for generic articles that can be found online.

## 93. How much do migration/visa/residence/citizenship consultations cost?

Prices vary widely by country and complexity. Examples from public fee pages:

- UK immigration solicitor consultations can be around £180–£250 per hour in some firms, while full applications can cost thousands of pounds.
- US family immigration services can involve government fees from around $1,000+ to $3,000+ depending on route, plus optional legal/service fees.
- Investor migration advisory can range from several thousand dollars to very high retainers, while actual CBI investments often start in the six figures.

Product implication: users need price transparency and route-level cost ranges.

## 94. How much do paid country reports, guides and checklists cost?

Typical consumer products range from low-cost PDFs/checklists ($10–$100) to premium reports ($100–$500+) and bespoke consultations ($200–$1,000+). Investment/tax planning reports can be much more expensive.

The market values specificity: “Portugal D8 visa checklist for a family from X country with remote income” is more sellable than “Guide to Portugal.”

## 95. Which services use subscription models?

- Nomads.com / Nomad List-style communities;
- digital nomad communities;
- premium relocation newsletters;
- B2B global mobility platforms;
- HR compliance tools;
- tax/legal update platforms;
- data APIs like cost-of-living datasets;
- relocation SaaS tools.

Subscriptions work when data changes frequently or community/network value is continuous.

## 96. Which services earn from legal consultations?

- Boundless/CitizenPath-style guided application platforms;
- Fragomen and enterprise immigration firms;
- country-specific visa agencies;
- investment migration firms;
- relocation marketplaces that refer users to lawyers.

## 99. What do companies pay for in employee relocation?

Companies pay for:

- visa/work permit processing;
- compliance risk reduction;
- employee experience;
- moving household goods;
- temporary accommodation;
- home search;
- school search;
- tax support;
- payroll and social security compliance;
- spouse/family support;
- destination services;
- tracking and reporting;
- policy consistency;
- speed of onboarding.

## 100. Which paid features do users consider truly valuable?

Most valuable paid features:

- personalised eligibility screening;
- verified document checklist;
- route comparison with cost/time/risk;
- official-source monitoring and alerts;
- tax-residence risk warning;
- real budget calculator;
- expert review;
- lawyer booking with transparent pricing;
- family scenario modelling;
- country shortlist engine;
- relocation timeline;
- community Q&A with verified answers.

## Block-level conclusion

The strongest monetisation is not generic content. It is personalised decision support, verified legal/tax updates, document workflows, expert access and B2B compliance.
