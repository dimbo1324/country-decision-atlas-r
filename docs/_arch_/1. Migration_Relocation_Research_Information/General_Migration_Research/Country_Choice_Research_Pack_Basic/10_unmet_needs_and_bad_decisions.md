# 10. Problems and Unmet Demand

## 101. What relocation information is most scattered?

The most scattered information:

- visa eligibility;
- document requirements;
- processing times;
- taxes;
- healthcare access;
- school rules;
- housing reality;
- neighbourhood safety;
- banking;
- local bureaucracy;
- community experience;
- citizenship path;
- family/spouse rules;
- real cost after arrival.

Users often need 20–50 sources to answer one country decision.

## 102. What data do people most often lack when choosing a country?

Missing data:

- true monthly budget by persona;
- post-tax income estimate;
- current visa processing reality;
- probability of approval;
- local office differences;
- hidden costs;
- quality of public services for migrants;
- school availability;
- healthcare waiting times;
- spouse employment likelihood;
- rental acceptance for foreigners;
- citizenship/practical naturalisation timeline.

## 103. Which relocation questions are hardest to solve alone?

Hardest questions:

- Which legal route fits my exact profile?
- Will I become tax resident?
- Can I legally work remotely?
- What happens if my visa is delayed?
- Can my family come?
- Can my spouse work?
- Will my profession be recognised?
- Can I access healthcare immediately?
- What budget is realistic?
- Is this country good for my children?
- Can I eventually get citizenship?
- Which advice is current?

## 104. Where do people most often encounter outdated information?

- SEO visa blogs;
- old YouTube videos;
- old Reddit threads;
- Telegram pinned posts;
- Facebook group files;
- agency pages not updated after law changes;
- embassy pages not aligned with immigration ministry pages;
- unofficial checklists;
- copied content across websites.

## 105. Where do people most often encounter contradictory advice?

- tax residence;
- digital nomad legality;
- proof of income;
- document apostille/translation;
- bank accounts;
- local registration;
- rental contracts;
- insurance;
- whether a lawyer is necessary;
- citizenship timelines;
- school access;
- “how much money is enough.”

Contradictions often arise because advice depends on nationality, income source, family status and city.

## 106. What mistakes do people often make when choosing a country?

Common mistakes:

- choosing by influencer content;
- underestimating rent;
- ignoring tax residence;
- confusing tourist stay with residence;
- assuming English is enough;
- ignoring spouse/children needs;
- overvaluing citizenship timeline claims;
- trusting one lawyer/agent without comparison;
- ignoring healthcare;
- moving without emergency fund;
- not checking rule changes;
- using average cost-of-living data instead of personal budget.

## 107. What mistakes do people make after moving?

Common mistakes:

- delaying local registration;
- missing visa renewal deadlines;
- not learning the language;
- signing bad rental contracts;
- not understanding tax obligations;
- staying in expat bubble;
- not building local credit/banking history;
- underinsuring health risks;
- failing to keep documents updated;
- choosing unsuitable neighbourhoods;
- assuming temporary lifestyle can become permanent.

## 108. What decisions do people make based on unverified information?

High-risk decisions:

- quitting a job before visa approval;
- buying property for residence;
- changing tax residence;
- moving family before school confirmation;
- choosing a visa based on YouTube advice;
- using unlicensed agents;
- ignoring local work restrictions;
- assuming citizenship is automatic;
- remote-working illegally on a tourist visa;
- transferring large savings without banking plan.

## 109. What data would people like to see in one place?

Users want:

- visa routes;
- eligibility;
- documents;
- processing time;
- government fees;
- lawyer/service cost;
- tax rules;
- cost of living;
- rent;
- safety;
- healthcare;
- schools;
- work market;
- business rules;
- banking;
- real migrant reviews;
- law-change alerts;
- citizenship path;
- comparison between countries;
- personalised recommendation.

## 110. What tools could help people choose faster and more confidently?

High-value tools:

- country comparison matrix;
- persona-based scoring;
- visa eligibility wizard;
- document checklist generator;
- real budget calculator;
- tax-residence warning engine;
- law-change tracker;
- community evidence map;
- official-source citation system;
- relocation timeline;
- family scenario simulator;
- risk register;
- expert marketplace;
- saved country shortlist;
- AI summary with source confidence.

## Block-level conclusion

The core unmet demand is confidence. People do not only need more content; they need a decision system that tells them what is current, what is official, what is anecdotal, what is uncertain and what applies to their exact profile.
