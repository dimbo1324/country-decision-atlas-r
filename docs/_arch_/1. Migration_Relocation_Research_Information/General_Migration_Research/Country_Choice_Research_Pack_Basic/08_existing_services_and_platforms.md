# 8. Existing Services and Platforms

## 79. What websites and apps do people use to choose a country?

Common tools:

- Numbeo and Expatistan for cost of living;
- Nomads.com for digital nomad city comparison and community;
- InterNations for expat surveys and community;
- Mercer for corporate cost/quality-of-living data;
- OECD, IOM, UN, World Bank for macro data;
- government immigration portals for visas;
- relocation companies for employee mobility;
- Reddit/Facebook/Telegram/YouTube for real experiences;
- Henley, Nomad Capitalist, Arton, Immigrant Invest for investment migration content.

## 80. Which sites compare cost of living?

- Numbeo: large crowd-sourced global cost-of-living database with city comparisons and indices.
- Expatistan: collaborative city cost comparison.
- Mercer: corporate-grade cost-of-living ranking for international assignees.
- Nomads.com: nomad-oriented cost estimates combined with weather, internet and community.
- Affordability calculators: newer tools increasingly include tax and rent assumptions.

Weakness: crowd-sourced prices can be outdated, biased toward expat consumption, or inaccurate for specific neighbourhoods.

## 81. Which sites compare quality of life?

- Mercer Quality of Living ranking;
- InterNations Expat Insider;
- OECD Better Life-style data;
- Numbeo Quality of Life index;
- Global Peace Index / safety datasets;
- healthcare and education rankings;
- country/city dashboards.

Weakness: quality of life is subjective. A high-ranked city may still be bad for a specific migrant if housing, visa, language or work access is poor.

## 82. Which sites help with visa and migration rules?

- official government immigration portals;
- embassy/consulate pages;
- EU immigration portals;
- IATA/Timatic for travel entry rules;
- Fragomen, KPMG, PwC, EY mobility updates;
- Boundless, CitizenPath and similar US-focused services;
- Deel/Remote/Localyze/Jobbatical blogs and tools;
- immigration law firm websites;
- country-specific expat forums.

Weakness: many SEO visa guides are outdated or written for lead generation.

## 83. Which sites help with taxes and business abroad?

- national tax authority sites;
- PwC Worldwide Tax Summaries;
- KPMG and EY tax guides;
- Deloitte international tax;
- Nomad Capitalist;
- offshore/tax planning firms;
- company formation providers;
- Stripe Atlas-style startup resources;
- local accounting blogs.

Weakness: tax advice is dangerously personalised; generic articles can mislead.

## 84. Which sites help find reviews from migrants and expats?

- InterNations;
- Reddit;
- Facebook groups;
- YouTube expat channels;
- country-specific forums;
- Trustpilot reviews of relocation/legal services;
- blogs and newsletters;
- Telegram/WhatsApp communities.

Weakness: survivorship bias, negativity bias, influencer bias and outdated anecdotes.

## 85. Which sites help digital nomads choose countries and cities?

- Nomads.com;

- Deel remote work visa guides;
- Remote/Remofirst/other EOR blogs;
- digital nomad YouTube channels;
- Reddit r/digitalnomad;
- coworking and coliving platforms;
- Airbnb/local rental groups;
- cost-of-living tools.

## 87. Which sites help track laws and political changes?

- government gazettes;
- parliament websites;
- legal databases;
- Fragomen/KPMG/PwC/EY immigration alerts;
- OECD policy chapters;
- EU Commission and EMN reports;
- news outlets;
- legislative trackers;
- civic/legal data platforms.

A migration decision platform could combine legal change tracking with user-facing explanations.

## 88. Which services are most popular in migration, relocation and country choice?

The market is fragmented:

- consumer research: Numbeo, Expatistan, Nomads.com, InterNations, YouTube/Reddit;
- legal/visa: law firms, Boundless/CitizenPath-style services, Fragomen;
- investment migration: Henley, Arton, Immigrant Invest, Nomad Capitalist;
- B2B mobility: Localyze, Jobbatical, Deel, Remote, Envoy Global, Cartus, Graebel, Crown;
- cost/quality corporate data: Mercer.

No single dominant platform covers legal rules, cost, taxes, community experience, country comparison and scenario planning in one verified workflow.

## 89. Which functions are most useful?

Useful functions:

- city/country comparison;
- cost-of-living calculators;
- visa eligibility screening;
- document checklists;
- country guides;
- community reviews;
- relocation timeline;
- tax residency warnings;
- expert consultation booking;
- school/healthcare/housing information;
- law-change alerts;
- saved scenarios;
- family/spouse modelling;
- employer relocation workflow.

## 90. Which functions are weakest or inconvenient?

Common weaknesses:

- outdated visa pages;
- weak source citations;
- no personalised scenario;
- no tax after-rent calculator;
- no family-specific modelling;
- no confidence level;
- no community verification;
- SEO content instead of decision tools;
- no explanation of legal uncertainty;
- no tracking of rule changes;
- no distinction between tourist stay, residence, PR and citizenship.

## Block-level conclusion

Existing tools are useful but specialised. The gap is a unified evidence-based relocation decision platform with official data, community signal, legal updates, cost/tax modelling and persona-specific comparison.
