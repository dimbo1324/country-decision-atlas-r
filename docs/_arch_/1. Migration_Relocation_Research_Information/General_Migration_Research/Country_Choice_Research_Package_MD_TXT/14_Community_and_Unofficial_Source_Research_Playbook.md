# Community and Unofficial-Source Research Playbook

The user explicitly requested not to limit research to official sources. This file explains how to collect unofficial evidence in a reliable way without confusing anecdotes with facts.

## 1. Why unofficial sources matter

Official sources tell you what the law says. Communities tell you what happens in practice: appointment delays, landlord behaviour, bank account issues, hidden costs, school queues, clinic access, police behaviour, neighbourhood safety, social isolation and emotional adaptation.

## 2. Main unofficial-source categories

### Reddit

Useful subreddits:

- r/IWantOut — pre-move advice and eligibility questions.
- r/expats — life abroad and long-term adaptation.
- r/digitalnomad — remote-work mobility, cost, visas and cities.
- r/AmerExit — U.S.-origin relocation interest.
- Country/city subreddits — local reality checks.

Strength: searchable, public, critical comments. Weakness: anonymous, biased toward English-speaking users, often harsh or unrepresentative.

### Telegram

Especially important for Russian-speaking relocation, post-Soviet migration, country-specific chats and fast rule updates. Telegram is useful for immediate practical information, but misinformation risk is high. Always record channel name, post date, author type, evidence link and whether a claim was verified officially.

### Facebook groups

Strong for families, local expat communities, school/housing advice and city-level adaptation. Weakness: repetitive questions, hidden advertising, agency spam and moderation bias.

### YouTube

Useful for emotional narratives, cost breakdowns, visual city impressions and “mistakes I made” content. Weakness: creators optimise for views, and legal/tax information becomes outdated quickly.

### Discord and WhatsApp

Useful for digital nomads, startup communities and city-specific groups. Harder to audit and cite. Treat as discovery channels, not authoritative sources.

## 3. Recommended coding framework for community evidence

For every community item, code:

- country;
- city;
- source platform;
- language;
- date;
- user persona: student, family, remote worker, founder, investor, refugee, local worker;
- topic: visa, housing, tax, jobs, healthcare, schools, safety, banking, social life;
- sentiment: positive, neutral, negative, mixed;
- claim type: legal, practical, emotional, price, rumour;
- verification status: official-confirmed, multiple-community-confirmed, single anecdote, unverified;
- freshness risk: low, medium, high.

## 4. Search queries for Reddit / Google

Use queries like:

- `site:reddit.com/r/IWantOut "move to Portugal" visa family`
- `site:reddit.com/r/expats "hard truth" "living abroad"`
- `site:reddit.com/r/digitalnomad "tax residency" "digital nomad visa"`
- `site:reddit.com "country A vs country B" expat`
- `site:reddit.com "Numbeo" "not accurate" "cost of living"`
- `site:reddit.com "what I wish I knew" "moving to"`

## 5. Search queries for YouTube

- `why I left Canada cost of living`

## 6. Telegram research method

For Telegram, create a manual sheet:

| Channel/chat | Language | Country | Members | Posts/week | Main topics | Ads/spam level | Useful? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---:|---:|---|---|---|---|

Then collect 50–100 posts per country and tag them with the coding framework above. Do not publish private messages or personal data.

## 7. Red flags in unofficial sources

- No date.
- No nationality/visa type mentioned.
- “My friend said”.
- Agency pushing one route without alternatives.
- Cost numbers without city/neighbourhood and household size.
- Legal claims without official link.
- Old pinned messages in fast-changing visa groups.
- Claims that confuse tourist stay, residence permit and tax residence.

## 8. Best way to use unofficial evidence in a product

Do not present community evidence as law. Present it as:

- “Commonly reported issue”;
- “Anecdotal community signal”;
- “Needs official verification”;
- “Reported by multiple users in 2025–2026”; or
- “High contradiction across sources”.

This will make the platform more trustworthy than competitors that either ignore community reality or present anecdotes as facts.
