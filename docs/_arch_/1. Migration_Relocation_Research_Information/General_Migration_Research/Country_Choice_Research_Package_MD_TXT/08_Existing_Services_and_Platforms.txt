# 8. Existing Services and Platforms

## 79. Which websites and apps do people use to choose a country?

People use Numbeo, Expatistan, Nomad List, InterNations, OECD Better Life Index, UNDP HDI, World Bank data, country forums, Reddit, YouTube, relocation blogs, government portals, immigration-lawyer websites and expat Facebook/Telegram groups. No single service fully solves the decision problem.

**Sources:** S14, S15, S20, S22, S33, S35, S36.

## 80. Which sites compare cost of living across countries?

The best-known are Numbeo and Expatistan. Nomad List is used heavily by digital nomads and includes cost, internet, weather and community factors. These tools are useful for rough comparison, but community users often complain that real costs differ by neighbourhood, lifestyle, nationality and housing access.

**Sources:** S25, S33, S35, S36.

## 81. Which sites compare quality of life across countries?

OECD Better Life Index, UNDP Human Development Index, Numbeo Quality of Life, InterNations Expat Insider and various country rankings compare quality of life. Official indexes are better for macro indicators; expat surveys capture lived experience but can be biased toward certain nationalities and income groups.

**Sources:** S14, S15, S20, S34, S37.

## 82. Which sites help with visas and migration rules?

Official government portals are the primary source. For summaries, people use immigration-lawyer websites, relocation agencies, Global Citizen Solutions, Henley & Partners, Nomad List visa pages, Citizen Remote-style lists, KPMG mobility alerts and Fragomen-type updates. The problem is that summaries often become outdated.

**Sources:** S30, S38, S39, S40, S41, S42, S43, S44.

## 83. Which sites help with taxes and business abroad?

People use Big Four mobility alerts, tax-advisory blogs, government tax authority pages, relocation platforms, founder communities, Stripe Atlas-type content, country startup portals and investment-migration firms. Reliable tax advice usually requires paid professional review because personal facts change the answer.

**Sources:** S48, S49, S50, S52.

## 84. Which sites help find migrant and expat reviews?

InterNations, Reddit, Facebook groups, YouTube, local expat forums, Trustpilot reviews of relocation agencies, country-specific Telegram groups and personal blogs are common. The strongest reviews are specific and recent: city, visa type, income level, family status, language ability and date.

**Sources:** S20, S22, S23, S24, S26.

## 85. Which sites help digital nomads choose countries and cities?

Nomad List is the most recognisable. Global Citizen Solutions publishes a digital-nomad visa index. MBO Partners provides U.S.-focused digital nomad data. Reddit r/digitalnomad, YouTube creators and remote-work visa lists are used for qualitative checks. Users compare cost, internet, safety, community, tax, visa and short-term rental access.

**Sources:** S24, S27, S28, S36.

## 86. Which sites help companies and HR departments organise employee relocation?

Companies use platforms and providers such as Localyze, Benivo, Deel, Remote, Topia, Jobbatical, Envoy Global, Fragomen, KPMG, WERC/WHR Global and work-from-anywhere compliance tools. The B2B need is compliance, immigration, payroll, tax, relocation logistics, employee experience and risk control.

**Sources:** S50, S51, S52, S53.

## 87. Which sites help track laws and political changes?

Official gazettes, parliament websites, government immigration portals, OECD policy reports, EU sources, law firms, KPMG/Fragomen alerts, Lexis/Nexis/Westlaw-style legal databases and country-specific legal-monitoring services. For a consumer platform, official-law tracking plus AI summaries could be a major differentiator.

**Sources:** S38, S39, S40, S41, S42, S43, S47.

## 88. Which services are most popular in migration, relocation and country choice?

For consumers: Numbeo, Expatistan, Nomad List, InterNations, Reddit, YouTube and government portals. For high-net-worth migration: Henley & Partners, Arton-style firms, Immigrant Invest and investment-migration consultancies. For employers: Localyze, Benivo, Deel, Remote, Topia, Jobbatical, Envoy Global and legal mobility providers.

**Sources:** S20, S33, S35, S36, S48, S50, S51, S52.

## 89. Which functions of these services are most useful?

Useful functions include cost comparisons, city rankings, visa summaries, community reviews, relocation checklists, government-rule links, employee case tracking, document workflows, tax/compliance alerts, and country scorecards. The most valuable feature is not raw data but decision support: “What does this mean for my exact case?”.

**Sources:** S20, S33, S36, S50, S51, S52.

## 90. Which functions are weakest or inconvenient?

Weaknesses include outdated visa data, unclear source dates, one-size-fits-all scores, poor legal disclaimers, weak comparison by persona, lack of community evidence reliability scoring, no “effective date” for law changes, and poor integration between official rules and lived experience. Many services are either too statistical or too anecdotal.

**Sources:** S25, S33, S35, S36, S50.
