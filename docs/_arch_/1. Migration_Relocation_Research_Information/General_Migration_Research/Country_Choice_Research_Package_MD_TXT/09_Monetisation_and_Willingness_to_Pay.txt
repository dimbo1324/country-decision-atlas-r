# 9. Monetisation and Willingness to Pay

## 91. What relocation information are people willing to pay for?

People pay for information that reduces risk: verified visa pathways, document checklists, tax summaries, country reports, school/housing guidance, legal eligibility screening, relocation budgets, healthcare access, citizenship timelines and personalised plans. Generic blog articles are weak monetisation; personalised, verified, date-stamped guidance is stronger.

**Sources:** S20, S48, S50, S51, S52.

## 92. What migration-related services do people already pay for?

They pay for immigration lawyers, visa agencies, relocation consultants, tax advisers, school consultants, property search, corporate relocation, language tutoring, document translation, apostilles, insurance, banking help, company formation, investment migration programs, and destination services.

**Sources:** S48, S50, S51, S52, S53.

## 93. How much do consultations on migration, visas, residence and citizenship cost?

Prices vary widely by country and complexity. Simple online consultations may cost tens to a few hundred dollars/euros. Serious legal consultations often cost several hundred per hour. Full visa packages can range from hundreds to thousands. Investment migration services can cost many thousands plus government fees and investment capital. Exact pricing must be sampled by country and provider.

**Sources:** S48, S50, S51.

## 94. How much do paid country reports, guides and checklists cost?

Paid guides often range from low-cost PDFs under $50 to premium reports or relocation plans costing hundreds. Corporate benchmark reports, legal databases and high-net-worth migration reports can be far more expensive or gated behind consultation funnels. Users pay more when the guide is current, actionable and tailored.

**Sources:** S48, S52, S53.

## 95. Which services use subscription models?

Nomad List uses membership/subscription-style access. B2B global mobility tools often use SaaS pricing. Legal intelligence, HR compliance, tax databases and relocation-management systems commonly charge recurring fees. Consumer subscription could work for alerts, personalised country watchlists, visa-change monitoring and community access.

**Sources:** S36, S50, S51, S52.

## 96. Which services earn from legal consultations?

Immigration law firms, investment-migration firms, relocation agencies, citizenship-by-investment consultancies, tax advisers and global mobility providers earn directly or indirectly from consultations. Many free guides are lead-generation tools for paid legal or relocation services.

**Sources:** S48, S49, S50, S51, S52.

## 97. Which services earn through affiliate programs?

Relocation content sites often monetise insurance, housing, money transfers, banks, international SIM/eSIM, legal consultations, visa services, language courses, coworking, tax software, moving companies and employer-of-record/payroll partners. Affiliate monetisation is attractive but can damage trust if recommendations are not transparent.

**Sources:** S50, S51, S52.

## 98. Which B2B global mobility and relocation services exist?

Key categories include immigration case management, relocation management, work-from-anywhere compliance, employer-of-record services, payroll, tax equalisation, assignment management, temporary housing, destination services, school search and employee experience platforms. Examples include Localyze, Benivo, Deel, Remote, Topia, Jobbatical, Envoy Global and consulting firms.

**Sources:** S50, S51, S52, S53.

## 99. What do companies pay for in employee relocation?

Companies pay for compliance, visa sponsorship, immigration filings, document workflows, relocation logistics, temporary accommodation, moving costs, tax advice, payroll setup, schooling support, spouse support, destination orientation, risk management, and employee retention. The business value is avoiding illegal work, failed relocations and talent loss.

**Sources:** S50, S51, S52, S53.

## 100. Which paid features do users consider truly valuable?

The most valuable features are eligibility checks, personal route comparison, document checklist, timeline, cost forecast, tax-risk summary, alerts on rule changes, lawyer-reviewed answers, country-fit scoring, and verified community insights. Users pay when the product saves time, prevents expensive mistakes, or gives confidence before a major life decision.

**Sources:** S20, S21, S22, S48, S50.
