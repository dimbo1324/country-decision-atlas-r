# Country and Segment Matrix

This file is not a legal recommendation. It is a strategic map of how different countries appear in migration and relocation research. Every country-specific rule must be verified through official sources before use.

## How to read the matrix

- **Appeal** = why people consider the country.
- **Typical personas** = who usually has a strong reason to compare it.
- **Risks** = what relocants often underestimate.
- **Policy direction** = broad direction as of 2026: opening, tightening, mixed or segment-specific.

## Strategic interpretation

1. **Classic high-income destinations** remain attractive but are under pressure from housing, politics and public-service capacity.
2. **Remote-work destinations** compete with lifestyle and cost, but tax and legal compliance are becoming more important.
3. **Investor destinations** are shifting away from easy real-estate routes toward higher compliance, productive investment and stricter due diligence.
4. **Emerging “balanced” destinations** may win if they combine safety, affordability, clear residence paths and reliable bureaucracy.
5. **No country should be ranked globally without persona context.** A country that is excellent for a remote worker can be poor for a local-job seeker or family with children.
