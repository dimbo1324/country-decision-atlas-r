# Product Opportunity Map — From Research to Platform Ideas

## 1. Core user problem

People are not suffering from a lack of information. They are suffering from **fragmented, outdated, contradictory and non-personalised information**. They need to know what is true for their nationality, budget, family status, income type, profession and timeline.

## 2. Strongest product thesis

A differentiated platform can combine:

- objective country data;
- official visa and policy tracking;
- AI summaries with source citations;
- community evidence with reliability scoring;
- legal/tax warning flags;
- personalised country matching;
- scenario simulation;
- country trajectory indicators: open/closed, liberal/authoritarian, business-friendly/hostile, low-tax/high-tax, easy/hard integration.

## 3. Highest-value personas

### Skilled professional

Needs: job market, visa path, salary, recognition, PR/citizenship path, family rights.

### Digital nomad / remote worker

Needs: legal remote-work status, tax risk, internet, rent, community, healthcare, duration of stay.

### Family with children

Needs: safety, schools, healthcare, housing, spouse work rights, stable residence, citizenship timeline.

### Student

Needs: tuition, university quality, work rights, post-study visa, PR path, housing.

### Founder / entrepreneur

Needs: company formation, bank account, tax, talent, startup visa, market access.

### Investor / high-net-worth individual

Needs: residence/citizenship optionality, asset protection, tax, banking, due diligence, family inclusion.

## 4. Features with highest willingness to pay

1. Visa eligibility calculator.
2. Personal country shortlist.
3. PR/citizenship timeline simulator.
4. Relocation budget calculator.
5. Tax residency red-flag report.
6. Document checklist by country and route.
7. Law-change alerts.
8. Expert-reviewed country dossiers.
9. Community pain-point heatmap.
10. “Reality check” comparing official data vs expat/community reports.

## 5. Trust model

Every data point should have:

- source URL;
- source type: official, survey, commercial, community;
- last checked date;
- confidence level;
- country applicability;
- user-type applicability;
- change risk.

## 6. Differentiation from existing platforms

Numbeo/Expatistan: good for cost, weak for law and personal route.

Nomad List: strong for nomads, weak for families, permanent migration and legal depth.

InterNations: strong survey/community signal, weak for visa calculation.

Government portals: authoritative but fragmented and hard to compare.

Lawyer sites: useful but narrow and often lead-generation oriented.

Global mobility platforms: strong for employers, not independent migrants.

A new platform can win by integrating these layers into one decision engine.

## 7. Suggested MVP scope

Start with 20–30 high-demand countries and 5 personas. Build country cards with:

- visa/residence routes;
- PR/citizenship timeline;
- cost-of-living range;
- tax-risk flags;
- healthcare/schooling notes;
- safety and quality-of-life indicators;
- law-change tracker;
- community sentiment summary;
- “best for / bad for” blocks;
- official-source links.

## 8. Data architecture idea

Use structured entities:

- Country
- City
- VisaRoute
- ResidencePath
- CitizenshipRule
- TaxRule
- CostMetric
- SafetyMetric
- HealthcareMetric
- EducationMetric
- CommunitySignal
- LegalSignal
- Source
- EvidenceItem
- Persona
- ScenarioRun

This matches the long-term idea of a Country Direction Atlas / country-comparison platform.
