# Methodology and Limitations

## Research approach

The research uses four evidence layers:

1. **Official quantitative baseline** — IOM, UN DESA, UNHCR, OECD, UNESCO, World Bank, Eurostat, UNDP and national immigration authorities. This layer is used for global migrant stocks, forced displacement, OECD migration flows, student mobility, remittances and official policy changes.
2. **Public opinion and demand signals** — Gallup, InterNations, public search-trend reports, Google Trends as a recommended follow-up tool, and open social platforms.
3. **Market and service intelligence** — global mobility platforms, relocation companies, investment-migration firms, digital nomad indexes and country-comparison services.
4. **Community evidence** — Reddit, YouTube topics, public expat articles and qualitative complaints. This layer is useful for discovering pain points but should not be treated as statistically representative.

## Confidence levels

- **High confidence**: supported by official statistical or government sources.
- **Medium confidence**: supported by credible surveys, market reports or several converging sources.
- **Low confidence / qualitative signal**: based on public community discussions, YouTube content, Reddit posts or commercial blog posts.

## Main limitations

- Migration definitions differ: “foreign-born”, “foreign citizen”, “long-term migrant”, “temporary resident”, “asylum seeker”, “refugee”, “student”, “digital nomad” and “expat” are not interchangeable.
- Global migrant stock changes slowly, but policy rules can change quickly, sometimes within months.
- Search-volume data is hard to compare globally because Google Trends uses relative indexes, not absolute global query counts.
- Telegram, Facebook and Discord communities may be very important for real user behaviour, but closed/private channels cannot be independently verified without direct access.
- Commercial relocation and investment-migration sources often have incentives to exaggerate demand. Use them as market signals, not neutral statistics.

## Recommended next research step

For a serious product-discovery phase, build a country-by-country evidence table with these columns: country, visa routes, official source URL, last checked date, community sentiment, cost-of-living reliability, tax risk, housing risk, healthcare risk, integration difficulty, and confidence score.
