### Q33. Is the number of search queries about moving abroad growing?

Yes — measurably so, with notable spikes tied to political events:

- After the **US 2024 presidential election result** (Trump's re-election in November 2024), Google searches for "move to Canada" spiked **1,270%** within 24 hours. Searches for moving to New Zealand rose **~2,000%**, and for Australia by **820%**.
- New Zealand's official immigration website logged **~25,000 new US users** on November 7, 2024, versus ~1,500 the same day the previous year.
- This pattern mirrors what happened after Trump's first election in 2016, though the 2024 spike was larger and more sustained.
- More broadly, Gallup data confirms a long-term upward trend in emigration intent in many countries, which typically correlates with increased online research.
- Academic research (Wanner, Bronitsky & Vakulenko) has validated that **Google Trends indices** for migration-related keywords reliably predict actual migration flows, confirming the behavioral connection.

Key takeaway: Search interest in emigration spikes dramatically around **political crises and elections**, but there is also a **steady secular increase** driven by remote work, rising cost of living, and growing awareness of migration options.

### Q34. Which migration-related search queries are growing fastest?

Based on available data from 2023–2025:

**Fastest-growing query categories:**
1. **Digital nomad visas** — searches for specific country digital nomad visa programs exploded (e.g., "Portugal digital nomad visa," "Spain nomad visa," "Thailand nomad visa").

6. **Country-specific visa questions** — "Portugal D7 visa," "Germany Blue Card," "Canada Express Entry 2025."
7. **"How to leave [country]"** — spikes dramatically in politically charged moments (US, Russia, UK post-Brexit).

### Q39. Is interest in digital nomad visas growing?

**Yes — dramatically.** Key indicators:
- In 2019, fewer than 5 countries offered a formal digital nomad visa; by 2025, **45+ countries** have launched dedicated programs.
- APAC alone saw a wave of launches: Indonesia, Japan, South Korea, Malaysia, Taiwan, and the Philippines all introduced programs 2024–2025.
- Turkey launched a nomad visa in April 2024; Italy in April 2024; South Korea's Workcation Visa launched in January 2024; Bulgaria launched its program in July 2025.
- Google searches for terms like "digital nomad visa Portugal," "Spain nomad visa," and country-specific nomad queries have grown consistently since 2021.
- The number of self-identified digital nomads globally is estimated at **35–40 million** (various 2024–2025 studies), with projections of continued growth.
- The Global Digital Nomad Report 2025 (Global Citizen Solutions) notes that digital nomad visa programs are "maturing" and "becoming institutionalized" — moving from a niche to a mainstream mobility category.

### Q40. Is interest in second passports and citizenship by investment growing?

**Yes**, and it has accelerated amid geopolitical instability:
- The investment migration industry has grown rapidly; Portugal's Golden Visa received a **record 4,987 applications in 2024**, up 72% YoY.
- Demand for Caribbean CBI programs (St. Kitts & Nevis, Dominica, Grenada, Antigua) remains high, especially among non-Western passport holders seeking visa-free travel.
- Interest has surged particularly from: Russian nationals (post-2022), Chinese HNWIs, American professionals (post-2024 elections), and Nigerians/Ghanaians.
- Key drivers: **passport strength** (visa-free access), **political hedge** ("plan B"), **tax optimization**, and **asset protection**.
- Searches for "second passport" and "citizenship by investment" have grown consistently according to investment migration firm data and Google Trends analysis.
- The EU's closure of Malta's CBI program (2022) and pressure on other programs has shifted demand to Caribbean, Vanuatu, Turkey, Jordan, and non-EU alternatives.

### Q41. Is interest in low-tax countries growing?

**Yes, substantially:**
- UAE (0% income tax) has become one of the most searched relocation destinations globally for high-income earners since the pandemic.
- Searches for "move to Dubai taxes," "UAE residency for freelancers," and related terms have grown significantly since 2021.
- **Georgia** (country) has seen massive interest — territorial tax system, easy residency, 1% small business tax — especially from digital nomads and entrepreneurs.
- **Bulgaria** (10% flat tax), **Romania** (10%), **Hungary** (15%), **Paraguay** (territorial tax) increasingly appear in "low tax country to live in" content and searches.
- The discontinuation of Portugal's NHR regime in 2024 pushed many to search for alternatives.
- Interest in **Panama** (territorial taxation, Pensionado program), **Costa Rica**, and **Malaysia's MM2H** program has grown among retirees and remote workers.

### Q42. Is interest in low cost-of-living countries growing?

**Yes — markedly so post-2022**, driven by global inflation and housing cost crises:
- **Vietnam** topped InterNations Personal Finance Index for 3+ consecutive years — consistently the most searched affordable high-quality expat destination in Southeast Asia.
- **Mexico, Colombia, Georgia, Albania, Indonesia, Thailand** all saw surges in search interest as English-speaking digital nomads sought affordable alternatives to expensive Western cities.
- The "geo-arbitrage" concept — earning Western wages while spending locally — has become a mainstream discussion topic rather than a niche one.
- Numbeo, the world's largest cost-of-living database, reports significant traffic growth on city and country comparison pages.
- Searches for "cheapest countries to live in with high quality of life," "affordable expat destinations," and related terms show consistent growth.
- **Housing crises** in Canada, Australia, UK, and major US cities directly drive searches for affordable international alternatives.
