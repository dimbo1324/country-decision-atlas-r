# 8. Existing Services and Platforms

## Key takeaways

- The market is fragmented: cost-of-living tools, expat forums, visa consultants, legal databases, nomad platforms, relocation vendors, HR mobility tools, tax advisors and country-ranking sites each solve part of the problem.
- No single mainstream platform fully combines: verified legal pathways + live policy direction + cost after tax/rent + real migrant experiences + scenario comparison + community evidence + government-law tracking.
- Strongest existing services are good at one layer, weak at cross-source verification and personalised country-choice decisions.

---

## 79. What websites/apps do people use to choose a country?

Common tools: Numbeo, Expatistan, Nomads.com/Nomad List-style sites, InterNations, Expatica, Expat.com, International Living, Expat Exchange, Teleport-style city comparison tools, government immigration portals, YouTube, Reddit, Facebook groups, Telegram channels, immigration-law firm sites, tax blogs, country indexes and relocation agencies [S29–S33, S41–S43].

## 80. Which sites compare cost of living?

Numbeo and Expatistan are the best-known crowdsourced cost-of-living comparison tools [S30–S31]. Other tools include Wise/Expatica guides, International Living cost articles, local real-estate portals, rent websites, supermarket price checks and salary calculators. Weakness: many tools use averages that do not reflect foreigner-accessible rentals, taxes, school costs, healthcare, deposits, agency fees, visas, or neighbourhood-specific reality [S44].

## 81. Which sites compare quality of life?

InterNations Expat Insider, Numbeo Quality of Life, OECD Better Life Index, Mercer quality-of-living/cost-of-living reports, Economist Intelligence Unit rankings, World Happiness Report, Global Peace Index and various expat/lifestyle publications. Each has methodology bias. InterNations is useful because it surveys expats directly; the 2025 edition included more than 10,000 expats from 172 nationalities across 46 destinations [S29].

## 82. Which sites help find visa and migration-rule information?

Official government portals are primary [S22–S23]. Secondary sources include immigration-law firms, Fragomen/BAL/KPMG/EY mobility alerts, Expatica, Immigrant Invest, Henley, Nomad Capitalist, VisaGuide, relocation agencies and country-specific immigration forums. Rule: use consultant sites for discovery, official sites for confirmation.

## 83. Which sites help find tax and business information abroad?

Sources include official tax authorities, Big Four mobility/tax alerts, local accounting firms, Nomad Capitalist, Expatica, government investment agencies, startup visa pages, Stripe Atlas-like resources, World Bank/IFC business data, OECD tax data, and local corporate-service providers. Weakness: tax content is often generic and not integrated with immigration status, days-in-country, CFC rules or home-country obligations.

## 84. Which sites provide migrant and expat reviews?

InterNations, Expat.com, Expat Exchange, Reddit, Facebook groups, YouTube, Trustpilot for agencies, Google Maps local reviews, Quora and country-specific forums. Migrant reviews are valuable for lived experience but biased: people with extreme positive/negative experiences post more often; legal facts can be wrong; and experience differs by nationality, income, gender, language and family status [S29, S41–S45].

## 85. Which sites help digital nomads choose countries and cities?

Nomads.com/Nomad List-style platforms, Remote Year, SafetyWing guides, digital-nomad visa lists, Reddit r/digitalnomad, YouTube nomad channels, coworking/café maps, Airbnb/Booking, internet-speed tools and tax-residence calculators [S15, S32, S43]. Strong features: cost, internet, weather, community, safety, visa notes. Weak features: legal accuracy, tax certainty, long-term suitability, local backlash, family needs.

## 86. Which sites help companies and HR departments relocate employees?

B2B platforms and providers include Localyze, Topia, Deel, Remote, Envoy Global, Fragomen, BAL, Cartus, Graebel, SIRVA, Crown Relocations, Aires and WHR Global [S34–S37]. They handle immigration, relocation logistics, compliance, payroll, tax, assignments, business travel, remote-work risk and employee support. These are enterprise tools, usually not consumer country-choice products.

## 87. Which sites help track laws and political changes?

Official gazettes, parliament websites, government portals, EU EUR-Lex, national immigration offices, OECD, IOM, UNHCR, MPI, Fragomen/BAL/EY/KPMG alerts, LexisNexis/Westlaw, FiscalNote, VLex, OpenStates-style tools, country-risk firms, news APIs and law-firm newsletters. Weakness: law tracking is fragmented and usually not connected to migrant personas or practical relocation decisions.

## 88. Which services are most popular in migration, relocation and country choice?

Popular consumer/service categories:
- Government portals.
- Immigration-law firms and consultants.
- Expat media: Expatica, InterNations, Expat.com.
- Cost tools: Numbeo, Expatistan.
- Digital-nomad tools: Nomads.com/Nomad List-style platforms.
- Investment migration: Henley, Arton, Immigrant Invest, Global Citizen Solutions.
- B2B mobility: Localyze, Topia, Deel, Remote, Fragomen, BAL.
- Social platforms: Reddit, Facebook, Telegram, YouTube.
Popularity differs by segment: HNWIs use advisers; students use agencies and YouTube; workers use official portals, job boards and Reddit; HR uses B2B mobility vendors.

## 89. Which functions are most useful in these services?

Most useful functions: visa eligibility check, cost-of-living comparison, city/country ranking, community Q&A, relocation checklist, document list, processing-time estimate, tax guide, school/healthcare overview, neighbourhood advice, legal updates, consultant booking, HR case management, and digital-nomad filters. For B2B: compliance workflows, employee tracking, payroll integration, case status, immigration document collection and assignment-cost estimation [S34–S37].

## 90. Which functions are weakest or inconvenient?

Weaknesses: outdated visa data, weak source citations, generic rankings, no personalised weighting, no scenario modelling, poor tax integration, shallow healthcare/school details, biased reviews, little legal-change tracking, no conflict-resolution between sources, poor localisation by nationality, and no real post-arrival cost model. Many platforms answer “What is nice?” rather than “Can this exact person legally and financially succeed there?”
