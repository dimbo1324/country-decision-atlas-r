# 12. Research Implications for a Country-Choice / Migration-Intelligence Platform

This file is not tied to a specific product brief. It extracts general implications from the research questions.

## 1. The strongest unmet need

The market does not need another generic “best countries” blog. It needs a trust layer over fragmented relocation information.

The strongest product thesis:

> People need a verified, current, personalised and comparable decision system for choosing countries, understanding legal pathways, estimating real costs, and detecting political/legal direction changes.

## 2. Core user personas

1. Budget relocator: wants affordability, legal stay and basic safety.
2. Skilled worker: wants job route, salary, PR and credential recognition.
3. Student: wants study + post-study + PR pathway.
4. Family: wants safety, schools, healthcare and long-term stability.
5. Digital nomad: wants legal remote work, tax clarity, cost, internet and community.
6. Entrepreneur: wants company formation, banking, tax, market access and founder residence.
7. Investor/HNWI: wants diversification, tax, passport/residence portfolio and safety.
8. Politically concerned migrant: wants safe legal exit, documentation and risk planning.
9. Retiree: wants healthcare, climate, cost, safety and residence.

## 3. Data layers a strong platform should combine

- Country profile.
- Visa/residence pathways.
- PR/citizenship timeline.
- Official source tracker.
- Legal-change tracker.
- Cost-of-living and rent.
- Tax model.
- Healthcare and school model.
- Labour-market fit.
- Safety and crime.
- Political/legal direction.
- Community evidence.
- Expert validation.
- User scenario simulator.
- Post-arrival checklist.

## 4. Source reliability model

Suggested reliability labels:

- A: Official government / law / court / official statistical source.
- B: International organisation / OECD / UN / IOM / World Bank / ILO.
- C: Recognised law/tax/mobility firm with date and author.
- D: Commercial platform with methodology.
- E: Public community lived experience.
- F: Unverified rumour / anonymous claim / old forum post.

Community content should never be treated as law, but it is extremely valuable for discovering hidden friction.

## 5. “Country attractiveness” should not be one score

A single score is dangerous. Better model:

- Legal feasibility score.
- Financial feasibility score.
- Career/business fit score.
- Family fit score.
- Safety/stability score.
- Adaptation difficulty score.
- Long-term status score.
- Rule-change risk score.
- Community pain score.
- Confidence level.

The platform should allow users to change weights.

## 6. High-value paid features

- Personalised country shortlist.
- Visa eligibility engine.
- Cost-after-tax/rent calculator.
- PR/citizenship timeline.
- Rule-change alerts.
- Document checklist.
- Expert-reviewed report.
- Community evidence digest.
- Scenario simulator.
- Family/student/entrepreneur modules.
- Country deterioration/improvement tracker.
