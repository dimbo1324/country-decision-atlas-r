# 8. Existing Services and Platforms

## 79. What websites and apps do people use to choose a country?

People use a fragmented stack: Google, YouTube, Reddit, Facebook groups, Telegram chats, Numbeo, Expatistan, Nomads.com, InterNations, Expat.com, government visa sites, immigration-law firm blogs, tax guides, real-estate portals, school-rating sites, job boards, relocation agencies and country-specific forums. There is no single dominant platform that reliably combines visa law, cost, tax, safety, real user experience and personal suitability.

## 80. Which sites compare cost of living across countries?

The most used are Numbeo and Expatistan. They provide city and country-level cost comparisons, rent estimates and user-contributed price data. Wise, WorldPopulationReview and other publishers often republish or summarise cost-of-living indices. The weakness is that crowdsourced data can lag reality and may not match expat consumption patterns, safe-area rents, international schools or private healthcare. Cost tools are useful for first screening, not final budgeting.

## 81. Which sites compare quality of life across countries?

InterNations, Numbeo quality-of-life indices, OECD Better Life Index, World Happiness Report, Mercer quality-of-living rankings, Economist/Monocle-style city rankings, Global Peace Index and country dashboards all help. Each index has bias: expat surveys reflect respondents, official indices may miss foreigner experience, and city rankings may be oriented toward corporate assignees. A serious comparison should combine objective indicators with migrant reviews.

## 82. Which sites help with visa and migration rules?

Official government portals are the primary source. Secondary sources include Fragomen, KPMG, EY, Deloitte, PwC, Henley & Partners, Global Citizen Solutions, Nomad Capitalist, VisaGuide-style portals, immigration-law firm blogs, Expat.com guides and digital nomad visa databases. Weakness: many pages are SEO-driven and may become outdated. Good services timestamp updates, cite official law and explain edge cases.

## 83. Which sites help with taxes and business abroad?

Big Four guides, EY/KPMG/PwC/Deloitte mobility updates, local tax-law firms, Tax Foundation, OECD tax data, government tax authorities, Nomad Capitalist, Global Citizen Solutions, Henley, offshore/company formation consultancies and expat-tax firms are common. Entrepreneurs also use Stripe Atlas-style company resources, local chambers of commerce and startup-visa pages. The weakness is that generic tax advice is dangerous: individual tax status depends on nationality, residence days, income source and company structure.

## 84. Which sites help find migrant and expat reviews?

Reddit, Facebook groups, InterNations, Expat.com, Internations forums, Nomads.com, YouTube, TikTok, local Telegram/WhatsApp groups, Google Maps reviews, school forums and country-specific expat blogs. Reviews are valuable for lived reality but often biased by lifestyle, income, neighbourhood, nationality and personality. The best approach is to compare official rules with many recent first-person accounts.

## 85. Which sites help digital nomads choose countries and cities?

Nomads.com/Nomad List is the best-known nomad comparison platform. Other resources include Remote Year, Citizen Remote, Digital Nomads World, Global Citizen Solutions digital nomad reports, EY digital nomad index, travel insurance providers, coworking directories and YouTube nomad channels. Nomad tools are strong on lifestyle, internet and cost, but weaker on tax, employer compliance, long-term residence and family suitability.

## 86. Which sites help companies and HR departments organise relocation?

B2B global mobility and immigration platforms include Localyze, Jobbatical, Deel Immigration, Remote, Topia, Equus, Graebel, Aires, Cartus, Sirva/BGRS, Santa Fe Relocation, Crown Relocations and KPMG/EY/Deloitte/PwC mobility practices. They help with visas, work permits, case management, tax, payroll, relocation logistics, vendor management and compliance. Companies pay for risk reduction and employee experience, not just information.

## 87. Which sites help track laws and political changes?

FiscalNote, Quorum, LexisNexis State Net, VLex, Westlaw, Bloomberg Government, LegiStorm, official gazettes, parliamentary databases, EU EUR-Lex, national legal portals and policy-monitoring services. For migration specifically, OECD policy reports, Fragomen alerts, government immigration updates and law-firm newsletters are practical. A gap exists for migration-specific, cross-country legal signal tracking that translates law changes into personal relocation impact.

## 88. Which services are considered most popular in migration, relocation and country choice?

Popular consumer-side services include Numbeo, Expatistan, InterNations, Expat.com, Nomads.com, Reddit communities, YouTube relocation channels, government visa portals and country-specific law-firm guides. B2B services include Deel, Remote, Localyze, Jobbatical, Topia, Fragomen, EY, KPMG and relocation-management companies. Investment migration users often encounter Henley & Partners, Arton Capital, Global Citizen Solutions and Nomad Capitalist.

## 89. Which functions are most useful in these services?

Useful functions include cost comparison, visa eligibility screening, document checklists, processing timelines, country rankings, city filters, tax explainers, user reviews, relocation case tracking, employer compliance workflows, reminders, official-source links and community Q&A. For companies, the most valuable functions are visa case management, compliance alerts, payroll/tax coordination, relocation vendor workflows and employee dashboards.

## 90. Which functions are weakest or inconvenient?

Weak points include outdated visa pages, shallow country rankings, lack of personalisation, no legal update timestamps, poor integration between visa/tax/cost/housing data, unreliable crowdsourced prices, SEO content without official citations, little support for families, and no clear distinction between tourist, nomad, residence and citizenship pathways. Community platforms are rich but noisy; official sites are authoritative but hard to understand; commercial guides are readable but sometimes biased toward sales.
