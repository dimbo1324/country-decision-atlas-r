# Migration, Relocation and Country-Choice Research Package

## What this package contains

This archive answers 120 general research questions about migration, relocation and country selection. The answers are written in English and divided into thematic Markdown files matching the original question blocks.

## Method

The research combines several evidence layers:

1. **Official statistical sources** — UN DESA, IOM, UNHCR, OECD, ILO, UNESCO, World Bank, IDMC.
2. **Government and policy sources** — Canada, Australia, Germany, EU/Reuters legal-policy reporting, official visa pages where available.
3. **Commercial and industry sources** — InterNations, Henley & Partners, EY, Deel, Localyze, Jobbatical, Nomads.com, Numbeo, Expatistan, FiscalNote, Quorum.
4. **Public community signals** — Reddit communities, public YouTube relocation themes, public expat/nomad content and media reporting about post-election relocation searches and migration-firm inquiries.

## Important limitations

This is a desk-research package, not a full ethnographic or API-based dataset. It does **not** scrape private Telegram channels, closed Facebook groups, paid communities, or full YouTube comment sections. Publicly visible community and media signals are used as qualitative evidence only. Immigration rules change quickly; every country-specific rule should be rechecked on the relevant government website before being used for legal or business decisions.

## How to use the files

- Use files **01–11** for the answer blocks.
- Use **12_source_index.md** to inspect source categories and URLs.
- Treat rankings and “best country” recommendations as contextual signals, not universal truth. The right country depends on budget, passport, family status, profession, tax situation, language, risk tolerance and time horizon.

## High-level executive conclusion

The global migration system is moving in two directions at once: absolute numbers of migrants and displaced people have grown, but many high-income destinations are tightening low-skill, asylum and student channels while selectively opening doors for high-skill workers, health workers, entrepreneurs, investors and remote workers. People’s interest in migration remains historically high, but the gap between “wanting to leave” and “being able to move legally, affordably and safely” is large. The strongest unmet demand is for a trustworthy, up-to-date, comparative, evidence-based country-choice layer that combines official law, real-world experience, cost, tax, safety, family needs, career prospects and personal adaptation risk.
