# Executive Synthesis: What the 120 Questions Reveal

## 1. The central market problem

Migration information is abundant but fragmented. Official sources are accurate but hard to use. Communities are practical but unreliable. Commercial sites are readable but often biased or outdated. Users need a decision-support layer that tells them not only “what the law says” but “what this means for someone like me.”

## 2. The strongest unmet demand

The strongest unmet demand is a **trustworthy country-choice operating system** combining:

- official immigration law;
- verified legal-change history;
- real processing experience;
- cost of living by lifestyle profile;
- tax residency and business rules;
- safety and healthcare;
- education and family suitability;
- community evidence;
- AI summaries with source confidence;
- scenario comparison.

## 3. Most valuable personas

The highest-value personas are families, skilled professionals, entrepreneurs, investors, international students, digital nomads and politically anxious middle-class people seeking a backup plan. Each group has different willingness to pay and different risk tolerance.

## 4. Best product wedge

A strong starting wedge would be: **country comparison + visa/legal change tracker + relocation scenario simulator**. This solves a real pain before building a full community. Community can come later as evidence and experience data.

## 5. Core product warning

Do not build another generic “best countries” directory. The market already has many rankings. The opportunity is in **freshness, personalisation, evidence hierarchy and decision confidence**.
