4. SEARCH DEMAND AND ONLINE INTEREST

Research date: 16 June 2026

4.1 Is search demand about moving abroad growing?

Broadly yes, but search interest is event-driven and term-specific.

Observed patterns:
- Searches for “move abroad”, “move to Canada”, “leaving the country”, “second passport”, “digital nomad visa”, “citizenship by descent” and “low tax countries” often spike after elections, wars, policy changes or economic shocks.
- Google Trends measures relative interest, not absolute search volume.
- Exact country-by-country search volumes require a Google Trends export or SEO tool dataset.

Best interpretation:
Search demand is a leading signal of relocation anxiety or curiosity. It does not prove actual migration, but it can predict future demand for information, consultations and relocation services.

4.7 Is interest in digital nomad visas growing?

Yes. Digital nomad visas became one of the most visible new migration-search categories after 2020.

Evidence:
- MBO Partners estimated 18.1 million U.S. digital nomads in 2024, up 147% since 2019.
- EY reported more than 40 jurisdictions with digital nomad or remote-work visa options by mid-2024.
- Global Citizen Solutions tracked 64 countries in its 2025 digital nomad report, with most programs launched after 2020.
- Search studies and media reports indicate extremely strong Google interest in digital nomad visas, especially when new countries launch programs.

Important nuance:
- “Digital nomad visa” is not one legal category globally.
- Some countries offer a dedicated visa; others offer freelancer, self-employment, visitor-with-remote-work permission, or temporary residence programs.
- Users need tax analysis, not just visa eligibility.
