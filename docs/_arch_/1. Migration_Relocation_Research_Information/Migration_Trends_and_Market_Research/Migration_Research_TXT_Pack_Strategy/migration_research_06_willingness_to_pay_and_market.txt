6. WILLINGNESS TO PAY AND MIGRATION MARKET

Research date: 16 June 2026

6.1 What migration information do people already pay for?

Users already pay for:
- Immigration lawyer consultations.
- Licensed immigration consultant reviews.
- Visa agency services.
- Document checklists.
- Document review before filing.
- Application form preparation.
- Translation and apostille support.
- Country reports.
- Tax residency consultations.
- Relocation consultations.
- School and neighbourhood selection.
- Housing search support.
- Business formation / founder relocation.
- Investment migration advice.
- Premium expat communities.
- Employer-sponsored relocation packages.

Why they pay:
- To reduce visa refusal risk.
- To avoid wrong-country decisions.
- To save time.
- To reduce anxiety.
- To understand hidden costs.
- To compare routes objectively.
- To avoid legal mistakes with tax, work rights or overstays.

6.2 Fast-growing paid service categories

Fast-growing categories:
- Visa-tech and immigration workflow platforms.
- Relocation management software.
- Global mobility SaaS.
- Employer of Record / remote hiring platforms with immigration support.
- Immigration compliance tools.
- AI document review and eligibility screening.
- Digital nomad visa services.
- Tax residency / low-tax relocation advisory.
- Investment migration advisory.
- Founder/startup relocation services.
- Premium country-selection reports.

Market evidence:
- Industry estimates place the expat relocation service market in the multi-billion-dollar range, with forecasts around 9%-12% CAGR depending on definition.
- Relocation management software and immigration management software are also forecast as growing categories.
- Global mobility leaders expect relocation volumes to rise or remain strategically important, even as some countries tighten rules.

6.3 How much do people pay to reduce migration risk?

Typical B2C price bands:
- Simple paid guide/checklist: USD/EUR 10-100.
- Premium country report: USD/EUR 50-300.
- One-hour immigration consultation: often USD/EUR 100-500 depending on country and lawyer.
- Tax residency consultation: often USD/EUR 200-1,000+.
- Visa application support: hundreds to several thousand dollars/euros.
- Relocation package: often USD/EUR 1,000-10,000+ depending on family size, housing, schools and destination.
- Investment migration advisory: thousands to tens of thousands, excluding investment amount and government fees.

Example observed pricing:
- Germany relocation/visa consultation services can charge around EUR 250 for consultation and around EUR 990 for specific package support.
- Government visa/residence fees may be much lower than service fees; users pay mainly for certainty and execution.

6.4 Existing B2C products for migrants and relocants

Existing product types:
- Nomad List: city/country comparisons for digital nomads; cost, internet, weather, community.
- Numbeo: user-contributed cost of living, rent, healthcare, traffic, crime and pollution.
- Expat.com: country guides, forums, jobs, housing and services.
- InterNations: expat community and Expat Insider survey.
- Visa agency websites: route-specific application help.
- Relocation boutiques: housing, documents, schools, registration.
- Tax advisory firms: tax residency and cross-border planning.
- Citizenship-by-investment firms: passport/residence programs.
- Country-specific Telegram/Facebook/Reddit communities.

Weakness:
Most B2C products are either lifestyle-first, community-first, legal-service-first or data-first. Few combine personalised legal eligibility, country choice, risk scoring, tax, cost, policy direction and verified user experience.

6.5 Existing B2B products

B2B products include:
- Global mobility platforms.
- Immigration case-management software.
- Relocation management dashboards.
- HR assignment management tools.
- Visa compliance tracking.
- Payroll/EOR platforms.
- Tax equalisation and assignment-cost tools.
- Employee relocation vendors.
- Corporate housing platforms.
- Destination service providers.

Buyers:
- HR departments.
- Global mobility teams.
- Immigration legal teams.
- Startups hiring internationally.
- Enterprises moving employees.
- Universities and research institutions.
- Hospitals and care-sector recruiters.

Pain points:
- Compliance risk.
- Visa delays.
- Employee experience.
- Cost forecasting.
- Tax and payroll complexity.
- Fragmented vendors.
- Policy changes.
- Housing and family integration.

6.6 Can the platform monetise analysis rather than raw information?

Yes. Raw information is abundant but low-trust and fragmented. Users pay for personalised interpretation.

High-value paid outputs:
- Personal country shortlist.
- Eligibility score by route.
- Visa refusal risk estimate.
- Cost-of-move estimate.
- First-year budget.
- Tax-residency risk summary.
- Citizenship timeline comparison.
- Family relocation readiness check.
- Document checklist by nationality and route.
- Legal change alerts.
- “What can go wrong” risk report.
- Lawyer-ready case brief.
- Employer sponsorship strategy.
- Country direction index.

Best monetisation model:
- Free discovery and country browsing.
- Paid personalised report.
- Paid route comparison.
- Paid document/risk checklist.
- Subscription for law/tax/policy alerts.
- Referral revenue from vetted lawyers/relocation/tax providers.
- B2B dashboards for HR and global mobility.

6.7 Strongest pricing hypothesis

Possible B2C pricing:
- Free: country database, basic comparisons, public articles.
- USD/EUR 19-49: basic personalised eligibility screen.
- USD/EUR 99-199: full country shortlist and risk report.
- USD/EUR 199-499: family/entrepreneur/tax-sensitive deep report.
- USD/EUR 20-50/month: legal-change alerts and country tracking.
- Higher-ticket referral: lawyer, tax advisor, relocation provider.

Users are most likely to pay when:
- They are close to making a decision.
- They face legal uncertainty.
- The cost of mistake is high.
- They have family or business complexity.
- They are comparing several countries.
- Rules recently changed.
