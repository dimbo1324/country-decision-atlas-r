9. OPPORTUNITIES FOR A MIGRATION DECISION PLATFORM

Research date: 16 June 2026

9.1 Questions users cannot solve quickly today

Hard questions users struggle to answer:
- Which country fits my exact profile, budget, nationality, profession and family situation?
- What legal route is realistic, not just theoretically possible?
- How much money do I need for the first year?
- Can I work legally from day one?
- Can my spouse work?
- What is the real path to permanent residence and citizenship?
- Which rules changed recently?
- Which country is becoming more open or more closed?
- What are the hidden tax risks?
- What are the most common reasons applications fail?
- Which country is best for my children, not just for me?
- Which online advice is outdated?
- Which risks are official facts and which are subjective opinions?

Missing or weak features:

- Law-change tracking.
- Tax-residence warning system.
- Country direction index.

9.4 Can you create a country interest index?

Yes. A “Country Interest Index” could combine:
- Google Trends relative search interest for “move to [country]”, “[country] visa”, “[country] digital nomad visa”, “[country] citizenship”, “[country] taxes”.
- SEO search volumes from tools such as Ahrefs/Semrush/DataForSEO.
- Migration flows and visa application numbers where available.
- Residence permit approvals.
- Student visa applications.
- Asylum applications.
- Reddit/Telegram/Facebook/YouTube activity.
- Growth of country-specific expat communities.
- News volume around visa changes.
- Lawyer/consultation demand if available.
- Relocation-provider lead data.

Output:
- Rising interest.
- Falling interest.
- Stable interest.
- Hype spike after event.
- Sustained demand.
- High interest but low legal feasibility.

9.5 Can you create a country direction index?

Yes. A “Country Direction Index” should measure whether a country is becoming:
- More open or more closed to migrants.
- More pro-talent or less pro-talent.
- More pro-business or less pro-business.
- More tax-friendly or less tax-friendly.
- More stable or less stable.
- More family-friendly or less family-friendly.
- More attractive for citizenship or less attractive.

Inputs:
- New visa laws.
- Caps and quotas.
- Salary thresholds.
- Family/dependent rules.
- Student-to-work rules.
- Settlement/citizenship timeline.
- Golden visa changes.
- Tax reforms.
- Banking/AML restrictions.
- Housing regulation.
- Labour law.
- Political freedom and rule of law.
- Anti-immigration rhetoric and election results.

9.6 Can you predict rising interest before it becomes obvious?

Yes, using early signals.

Early signals:
- New digital nomad/startup/talent visa announcement.
- Sudden Google Trends spike for “move to [country]”.
- YouTube videos about the country gaining fast views.
- Telegram/Reddit group growth.
- Political crisis in an origin country.
- Election result triggering emigration searches.
- Currency crash or inflation spike.
- New tax regime for foreigners.
- Housing affordability changes.
- Airline route expansion.
- Visa-free travel change.
- High-profile influencer/creator relocation.
- Major employer recruitment campaigns.

Prediction model idea:
- Combine search interest + legal opening + affordability + social activity + press coverage + visa approval data.
- Detect divergence: online interest rising before official migration stock rises.

9.7 Minimum data users need for faster decisions

Minimum country-decision dataset:
- Can I legally enter?
- Can I legally stay?
- Can I legally work?
- Can my family join and work/study?
- What documents are required?
- How long does it take?
- What does it cost officially and realistically?
- How much first-year budget do I need?
- What taxes apply?
- Can I renew?
- Can I get PR/citizenship?
- How safe is it for my profile?
- Can I find housing?
- Can I access healthcare/schools?
- What are the top failure risks?
- What changed recently?

9.8 User outputs after using the platform

The user should receive:
- Top 3-7 country shortlist.
- Score for each country by profile.
- Best legal pathway per country.
- Eligibility explanation.
- Required document list.
- Estimated timeline.
- Government fees and realistic service costs.
- First-year budget estimate.
- Tax-residence warning.
- Family/spouse/children analysis.
- Safety and quality-of-life summary.
- PR/citizenship path.
- Country direction summary: opening/closing/stable.
- Main risks and failure points.
- Next action plan.
- Official source links.

9.9 What must be evidence-based, not subjective

Must be evidence-based:
- Visa eligibility.
- Required documents.
- Fees.
- Processing timelines where official data exists.
- Work rights.
- Family rights.
- PR/citizenship rules.
- Tax residence rules.
- Healthcare access rules.
- School enrolment rules.
- Criminal/safety statistics.
- Housing costs where data exists.
- Employment rules.
- Business formation steps.
- Legal changes.

Can be subjective but should be labelled:
- Lifestyle satisfaction.
- Friendliness.
- Food/weather preferences.
- Ease of making friends.
- Cultural fit.
- Bureaucracy experience.
- “Best city” opinions.
- Personal safety feelings.

9.10 How to be unique vs Nomad List, Numbeo, Expat.com or Telegram

Do not compete as a generic community or cost-of-living website. Compete as a decision and risk engine.

Unique features:
1. Personalised country matching by nationality, profession, income, family, budget and goals.
2. Legal route engine with official-source citations.
3. Country direction index: is the country opening or closing?
4. Country interest index: is demand rising or falling?
5. Risk score by route: refusal, tax, housing, job, safety, policy-change risk.
6. Citizenship timeline simulator.
7. Tax-residence warning layer.
8. Family migration module.
9. Digital nomad legality and tax module.
10. Verified user cases, not anonymous anecdotes only.
11. Law-change alerts and update history.
12. Early-signal dashboard for emerging destinations.
13. “What can go wrong” report generated for each profile.
14. Lawyer-ready case summary.
15. Provider marketplace with quality control and conflict-of-interest disclosure.

9.11 Recommended MVP

MVP structure:
- 20-30 most searched countries first.
- 5-8 user personas: skilled worker, remote worker, founder, student, family, retiree, investor, second-passport seeker.
- Core country database with visa, tax, cost, safety, citizenship and law-change tracking.
- Personalised quiz.
- Country shortlist output.
- Paid deep report.
- Official links and last-checked dates.
- User case collection.

MVP countries to consider:
- Canada, USA, UK, Germany, Australia, New Zealand, Spain, Portugal, Italy, Greece, UAE, Singapore, Mexico, Costa Rica, Panama, Uruguay, Argentina, Colombia, Thailand, Japan, Netherlands, Ireland, France, Estonia, Poland, Türkiye, Georgia.

9.12 Recommended long-term product vision

Build a migration intelligence operating system:
- For users: country choice, risk reduction and action plan.
- For lawyers: pre-qualified leads and structured case briefs.
- For relocation providers: user intent and route matching.
- For HR: global mobility risk dashboard.
- For investors/founders: jurisdiction comparison.
- For researchers/media: migration interest and country-direction indices.

The strongest moat will be structured, updated, source-cited data plus personalised analysis, not content volume alone.
