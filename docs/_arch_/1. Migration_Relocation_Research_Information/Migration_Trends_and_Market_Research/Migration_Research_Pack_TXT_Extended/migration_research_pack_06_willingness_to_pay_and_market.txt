6. WILLINGNESS TO PAY AND MARKET OPPORTUNITY

6.1 What migration information are people already willing to pay for?

People do not usually pay for generic information. They pay for risk reduction, personal interpretation, speed, confidence, paperwork, and accountability.

Paid categories:
1. Lawyer consultations.
2. Visa eligibility assessment.
3. Document checklists.
4. Application preparation.
5. Full visa packages.
6. Tax-residency consultation.
7. Business setup and company formation.
8. School search and family relocation help.
9. Housing search.
10. Bank-account setup.
11. Relocation concierge services.
12. Premium country reports.
13. Immigration compliance for employers.
14. Global mobility dashboards for HR.
15. Alerts about law changes.
16. Personalised country shortlists.

6.2 Current price signals

Examples from public pricing pages:

B2C / individual legal and visa pricing:
- MigRun advertises EU residence-permit support from about EUR 530.
- Dutch immigration-law examples: highly skilled migrant package around EUR 700, DAFT route around EUR 950, DAFT with family around EUR 1,250, excluding VAT.
- German immigration-law consultation examples: around EUR 109 for a short consultation and around EUR 226 for a more complete first consultation, with further hourly rates.
- UK immigration-law examples: several work and specialist visa services listed around GBP 2,500-5,000 depending on route.
- Australian migration-law consultation examples: around AUD 330-440 for consultations.

B2B / employer and relocation pricing:
- RelokateHR lists immigration/relocation cases from around EUR 950.
- Localyze lists specific UK-related employer services such as Certificate of Sponsorship issuing and priority support fees.
- Global mobility providers such as Deel, Localyze, Jobbatical, Remote, and others sell immigration, payroll, compliance, and relocation workflows to companies.

Interpretation:
Users already pay meaningful amounts when the task is legal, personal, high-stakes, or document-heavy. There is room below lawyers for software-guided paid assessments and above generic content for expert-reviewed reports.

6.3 Which paid services are growing fastest?

Fast-growing or strategically important categories:
1. Visa-tech platforms
Digital products that automate eligibility, document collection, workflow, and case tracking.

2. Employer global mobility SaaS
Tools for HR teams hiring international talent, sponsoring visas, tracking compliance, and managing relocations.

3. Immigration compliance
Companies need to avoid fines, illegal work, and status problems.

4. Remote-work compliance
As remote work crosses borders, employers and workers need tax, social-security, permanent-establishment, and work-authorisation checks.

5. Relocation concierge
Housing, school, banking, pets, moving logistics, registration, and settling-in.

6. Investment migration advisory
High-ticket but politically sensitive. Demand exists, but EU pressure and programme closures increase regulatory risk.

7. AI migration assistants
Emerging category: personalised eligibility checks, route comparison, document checklists, law-change alerts, and country scoring. This category must handle legal-advice boundaries carefully.

6.4 How much are people willing to pay to reduce the risk of choosing the wrong country?

Likely willingness-to-pay by segment:

Low-budget migrant / student:
- Free to EUR 50 for guides, checklists, and community access.
- EUR 50-200 for personalised assessments if trusted and source-cited.

Remote worker / freelancer:
- EUR 50-300 for visa/tax/country comparison.
- EUR 300-1,000+ if tax residency, company setup, or family complexity is involved.

High-skilled worker:
- EUR 100-500 for pathway assessment, document roadmap, and country shortlist.
- Higher if employer sponsorship and legal review are included.

Family:
- EUR 200-1,000 for school, healthcare, visa, tax, and housing-risk analysis.
- Families have high willingness to pay because mistakes affect children and long-term stability.

Entrepreneur:
- EUR 500-3,000+ for business/tax/residence analysis.
- Higher if company formation and banking are included.

Investor/HNWI:
- EUR 5,000-50,000+ for residence/citizenship, tax, inheritance, family office, and legal structuring.

B2B employer:
- Hundreds to thousands per case, plus platform subscriptions, depending on employee seniority and country complexity.

6.7 Can a platform monetise personal analysis rather than information?

Yes. This is the strongest opportunity.

Why personal analysis is monetisable:
- Generic information is abundant and often free.
- Laws are fragmented and change often.
- Users do not know which facts apply to them.
- Mistakes are expensive: rejected visa, tax liability, wrong school choice, bad city, failed job move.
- Users trust systems more when they can see dated sources and official references.

Paid product examples:
1. Personal country shortlist.
2. Eligibility score by visa route.
3. Risk report.
4. Cost-of-move estimate.
5. Tax-risk warning.
6. Family-fit report.
7. Document roadmap.
8. Law-change alerts.
9. Lawyer-reviewed premium report.
10. Employer-ready relocation plan.

Best monetisation model:
- Free: country pages, basic comparison, public data.
- Paid low tier: personalised quiz + shortlist + basic risk score.
- Paid mid tier: detailed PDF/TXT report, route plan, cost model, document checklist.
- Paid premium: expert/lawyer review, tax review, family plan, relocation concierge.
- B2B: HR dashboard/API for candidate country fit, visa feasibility, compliance warnings.

6.8 Pricing strategy for the platform

Suggested B2C pricing:
- Free country explorer.
- EUR 19-49: basic personalised report.
- EUR 99-199: detailed country shortlist with route and risk scoring.
- EUR 299-799: family/entrepreneur/remote-worker deep report.
- EUR 1,000+: expert-reviewed case with partner lawyer/tax advisor.

Suggested B2B pricing:
- Per employee: EUR 100-500 for initial mobility feasibility.
- Per active case: EUR 500-2,000 depending on complexity.
- Monthly SaaS: EUR 500-5,000+ depending on company size.
- API/data product: priced by seat, country coverage, or query volume.

6.9 What users are really buying

They are buying:
- Certainty.
- Personal relevance.
- Freshness.
- Official-source verification.
- Fewer mistakes.
- Time saved.
- A realistic path.
- Confidence to act.

This is more valuable than another generic "best countries to live" article.
