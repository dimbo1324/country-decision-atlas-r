6. Willingness to Pay and Market Evidence

Main answer
People already pay for migration information and execution when the risk of error is high. The strongest paid categories are legal consultations, visa filing, relocation packages, tax planning, school search, housing support, investment migration advice, employer immigration compliance, and global mobility software.

Important caveat
There are few reliable public surveys saying “people are willing to pay exactly X for country-selection advice.” The best available evidence is revealed willingness to pay: published lawyer fees, relocation package prices, subscription communities, market-size growth and B2B SaaS spending.

1) What migration information people already pay for
- immigration lawyer consultations;
- visa eligibility assessments;
- full visa application preparation;
- document checklist packages;
- apostille/translation coordination;
- tax residency consultations;
- citizenship by descent document research;
- golden visa / investment migration advice;
- relocation packages;
- school search;
- housing search;
- bank account setup;
- expat health insurance advice;
- employer-sponsored immigration compliance;
- country reports and premium guides;
- paid digital nomad communities and destination databases.

2) Market growth signals
- Expat relocation service market: estimated at USD 4.9 billion in 2024 and projected to reach USD 11.6 billion by 2034, according to InsightAce Analytic.
- Cross-border workforce and migration solutions: estimated at USD 3.8 billion in 2024, projected to USD 11.6 billion by 2034, according to Global Market Insights.
- Mobility and relocation management software: Mordor Intelligence estimates USD 4.50 billion in 2025 and USD 10.09 billion by 2031, with a CAGR around 14.58%.
These market estimates differ by methodology, but all point to growth driven by regulatory complexity, remote work, global hiring and talent shortages.

3) Pricing proxies
Published fees show the willingness-to-pay floor:
- Netherlands immigration lawyer fixed fees: examples include about EUR 700 for a highly skilled migrant worker permit, EUR 800 with family, and EUR 950-1,250 for Dutch-American Friendship Treaty cases, excluding VAT.
- UK immigration lawyer fees: published examples include GBP 2,500-5,000 for multiple work and mobility visa categories.
- U.S. immigration consultation fees: published legal-market guides often cite USD 75-250 for an initial consultation, with full representation much higher.
The implication: a user may resist paying USD 20 for generic content, but may pay USD 100-500 for personalised risk reduction before spending thousands on legal fees and relocation costs.

4) Fastest-growing paid services
Likely fastest-growing categories:
- B2B global mobility software;
- immigration compliance platforms;
- employer-of-record / remote hiring + visa support bundles;
- digital nomad tax and compliance advice;
- investment migration advisory under stricter due diligence;
- student visa and post-study work planning;
- citizenship by descent research;
- AI-assisted visa eligibility screening;
- relocation concierge services for high-income families.

5) Existing B2C products
- Nomads.com / Nomad List: city rankings, cost, internet, climate, community.
- Expat.com: country guides, forums, housing, jobs, service providers.
- InterNations: expat community, surveys, networking.
- Numbeo-style platforms: cost of living, safety, healthcare and quality of life based heavily on crowdsourced data.
- Country-specific relocation agencies.
- Immigration law firm assessments.
- Digital nomad visa directories.
- Paid newsletters / Substack relocation guides.

6) Existing B2B products
- Deel Mobility: global mobility, payroll, visa support and HR workflows.
- Envoy Global: immigration services for employers.
- Newland Chase / CIBTvisas: corporate mobility and immigration services.
- Jobbatical, Localyze, Topia, Equus, Fragomen platforms and similar global mobility systems.
Core B2B needs: compliance, case tracking, assignment management, tax coordination, employee experience, document management, reporting and risk control.

7) Can the product monetise analysis rather than raw information?
Yes. Raw information is commoditised; personalised interpretation is valuable. The winning monetisable outputs are:
- personalised country shortlist;
- route eligibility score;
- risk report;
- cost estimate;
- legal-change warning;
- family suitability report;
- tax/banking red flags;
- document readiness score;
- “avoid these mistakes” based on similar cases;
- lawyer/agent handoff with structured brief.

8) Strong B2C price ladder
Possible pricing model:
- Free: country browsing, basic guides, basic index.
- USD 15-30/month: saved comparisons, alerts, premium data, community.
- USD 49-99: one personalised country-fit report.
- USD 149-299: deep migration route and risk report.
- USD 499-1,500: concierge planning bundle with human expert review.
- Referral/affiliate: vetted lawyers, tax advisers, insurance, relocation, schools, banking.

9) Strong B2B price ladder
- SMB subscription: employee relocation decision support and compliance alerts.
- HR dashboard: countries, visas, timelines, document tracking, risk rules.
- Enterprise: API, immigration compliance integrations, mobility analytics, law-change monitoring, employee self-service.

10) Biggest commercial risk
Trust. Users will not pay for black-box advice in a legal/financial domain unless the platform shows sources, confidence, date of verification, and what is not legal advice.
