4. Search Demand and Online Interest

Main answer
Search interest in migration-related topics is clearly high, but exact query-by-query measurement requires Google Trends or Google Trends API access. Public evidence shows especially strong growth around digital nomad visas, second passports, fast residence, moving to Portugal/Spain/Canada, low-tax countries and remote-work relocation.

Important caveat
This file separates hard evidence from required future measurement. Google Trends gives normalised search interest, not absolute search volume. For a production platform, exact data should be collected systematically using the Google Trends API alpha, third-party SEO tools, YouTube search data, Reddit data, Telegram/Discord/community data, and country-specific search engines where relevant.

1) Are searches about moving abroad growing?
Evidence suggests yes for many countries and topics, with spikes around political events, new visa launches and rule changes. Examples:
- “digital nomad visa” searches reportedly hit a global peak in 2024 and rose dramatically around Italy’s 2024 visa launch.
- “move to Portugal” and Portugal-related relocation searches rose strongly among Americans around U.S. political events.
- Student-search data in 2025 suggests demand is shifting away from the Big Four destinations toward more countries in Europe and Asia.

High-interest relocation countries appear to include:

- Canada: permanent residence, skilled migration, education, safety;
- Australia: skilled migration, study, lifestyle;
- Germany: work, study, EU Blue Card, engineering/IT;
- Netherlands: highly skilled migrant, DAFT for Americans, tech;
- UAE: business, tax, remote work, finance, safety;
- Mexico: Americans, retirees, remote workers, cost/lifestyle;
- Thailand/Malaysia/Vietnam: affordability, nomads, retirement/lifestyle;
- Georgia/Armenia/Serbia/Montenegro: easier stay, lower cost, post-2022 relocation;
- Uruguay/Argentina/Paraguay/Panama/Colombia: Latin America alternatives, residence, affordability, lifestyle.

7) Digital nomad visa demand
Digital nomad visa interest has grown sharply since 2020. OECD noted that DNVs appeared in OECD countries around 2020 and spread quickly. A 2022 OECD count found 6 OECD and at least 22 non-OECD countries with specific DNV-style visas. Private 2025-2026 lists often count 45-70 countries, depending on definitions. MBO reports 18.5 million U.S. digital nomads in 2025, up 153% since 2019.

8) Fast residence / fast citizenship interest
Interest is rising because uncertainty is rising. People are searching not only for better lifestyle but also for optionality: safe second residence, backup plan, citizenship by descent, naturalisation timelines, and passport strength. However, EU investment routes are tightening, so “fast” options increasingly require trade-offs: high investment, real stay, language, integration, active business, or non-EU jurisdictions.

Platform implication
A unique platform can build a Country Interest Index using:
- Google Trends normalised search index;
- SEO volume by query cluster;
- YouTube video growth by country/topic;
- Reddit post/comment velocity;
- Telegram/Discord channel growth;
- visa application/approval volumes where available;
- official migration flow changes;
- media mentions;
- law-change announcements;
- political crisis signals.

This can reveal emerging destinations before mainstream media does. Early signal examples:
- sudden growth in “move to X” searches;
- new digital nomad visa launch;
- spikes in Reddit/Telegram questions;
- YouTube creators moving to X;
- housing/rent arbitrage trend;
- political shock in source country;
- new tax regime or startup visa;
- visa tightening in competing destinations.
