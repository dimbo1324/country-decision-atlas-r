10. Sources and Methodology

Methodology
This research pack uses a mixed evidence approach:
1. Official statistics: UN DESA, IOM, OECD, UNHCR, ILO, UNESCO, national governments.
2. Survey data: Gallup World Poll and related Gallup migration indexes.
3. Policy evidence: government and OECD immigration policy summaries, UK/Canada/Australia/NZ/EU sources.
4. Market evidence: relocation services, global mobility software and immigration services market estimates.
5. Platform/behavioural evidence: migration communities, expat platforms, digital nomad platforms and academic research on migrant information seeking.
6. Search-interest evidence: public Google Trends references and Google Trends API announcement. Exact keyword time series should be collected separately using the API or SEO tools.

Limitations
- International migrant stock is not the same as annual migration flow.
- Migration desire is not the same as concrete migration intent.
- Forced displacement and voluntary migration must be interpreted separately.
- Search trends are normalised, not absolute volume.
- Telegram/Facebook/WhatsApp community counts are not consistently auditable without platform/API access.
- Market-size reports use different definitions; treat them as directional evidence, not exact truth.
- Migration law changes frequently; all legal facts need ongoing verification before user-facing recommendations.
