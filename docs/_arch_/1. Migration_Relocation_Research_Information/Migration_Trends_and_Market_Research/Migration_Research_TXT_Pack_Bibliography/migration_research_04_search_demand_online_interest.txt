4. SEARCH DEMAND AND ONLINE INTEREST
Research date: 16 June 2026

4.1 Is the number of search queries about moving abroad growing?

Answer: The long-term online interest is structurally high, but Google Trends data must be read carefully because it shows relative interest, not absolute query volume.

Strong evidence exists for event-driven spikes:
- After the 2024 US election, Google searches for “move to Canada”, “move to New Zealand”, and “move to Australia” rose dramatically according to Reuters and Sky News reporting based on Google Trends data.
- Digital nomad visa searches rose strongly after 2020 as countries launched remote-worker visa programmes.
- Search demand for second passports, citizenship by descent, low-tax countries, and residence permits tends to spike after political, tax, war, banking, or election shocks.

Important caveat:
Google Trends is excellent for relative momentum and early signals. It is not a direct count of people who will migrate.

- “best countries to live”.

Interpretation:

Platform implication:
A risk engine should answer fear searches directly, using evidence and severity labels. This is more valuable than a generic “best countries” ranking.

4.7 Is interest in digital nomad visas growing?

Answer: Yes, strongly since 2020.

Evidence:
- OECD notes that special digital-nomad statuses began appearing in OECD countries around 2020 and continued spreading.
- EY reported that more than 40 jurisdictions had digital nomad or remote-worker permit schemes by the end of July 2024.
- Other public trackers and professional reports identify more than 60 digital-nomad schemes by 2025.
- MBO Partners estimated 18.5 million US digital nomads in 2025, up about 153% since 2019.

Popular digital-nomad destinations:
- Portugal, Spain, Italy, Greece, Croatia, Estonia, Malta.
- UAE/Dubai.
- Mexico, Costa Rica, Panama, Colombia, Brazil, Argentina, Uruguay.
- Thailand, Indonesia/Bali, Malaysia, Japan, South Korea, Taiwan.
- Georgia, Albania, Montenegro.

4.8 Is interest growing in fast residence, permanent residence, or citizenship?

Answer: Yes, especially among users who want optionality, risk reduction, better passports, or long-term family planning.

High-demand search themes:
- fast residency.
- easy residence permit.
- permanent residence after 5 years.
- citizenship after 3/5/7/10 years.
- citizenship by descent.
- citizenship by investment.
- EU passport ancestry.
- golden visa.
- naturalisation requirements.
- dual citizenship allowed.

However, “fast” routes are becoming more regulated. A platform must distinguish between marketing claims and legal reality.

Recommended search-data system for a platform

Track weekly, by origin country and language:
1. Generic intent: move abroad, immigrate, relocate, emigrate.
2. Country-specific intent: move to Portugal, move to Canada, etc.
3. Legal-pathway intent: visa, residence permit, permanent residence, citizenship.
4. Segment intent: digital nomad, startup visa, skilled worker, student visa, retirement visa.
5. Risk intent: crime, tax, visa rejection, bureaucracy, racism, healthcare.
6. Comparison intent: Portugal vs Spain, Canada vs Australia, etc.
7. Shock intent: after elections, wars, tax changes, mobilisation, banking restrictions.

Use search demand as an early signal, not as proof of migration. Combine it with visa applications, law changes, migration statistics, job postings, social media activity, and community growth.
