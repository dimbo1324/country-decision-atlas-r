6. WILLINGNESS TO PAY AND MARKET LANDSCAPE
Research date: 16 June 2026

6.1 What migration information are people already willing to pay for?

People already pay for:
- Immigration lawyer consultations.
- Visa eligibility assessments.
- Full visa application support.
- Document checklists and document review.
- Translation, apostille, and notarisation support.
- Tax-residency planning.
- Second-passport and citizenship-by-descent assessments.
- Golden visa / investor residence advisory.
- Startup visa and business immigration support.
- Relocation packages.
- Housing search.
- School search.
- Healthcare insurance setup.
- Bank-account opening support.
- Company formation.
- Expat community memberships.
- Country reports and premium relocation guides.
- Employer-sponsored global mobility services.

Why they pay:
They are not paying only for information. They are paying to reduce uncertainty, avoid rejection, save time, avoid tax/legal mistakes, and make a high-stakes life decision with confidence.

6.2 Which paid services are growing fastest?

Fast-growing categories:
- Visa tech and immigration workflow platforms.
- Corporate global mobility SaaS.
- Employer-of-record and cross-border employment platforms.
- Relocation management software.
- Immigration compliance tools.
- Digital nomad and remote-worker visa services.
- Investor migration and residence diversification advisory.
- Tax-residency and international structuring services.
- AI-assisted legal intake and document preparation.

Market estimates from research firms:
- Immigration consulting services market: estimated around USD 18.01 billion in 2024, projected to reach about USD 34.285 billion by 2032, according to Credence Research.
- Mobility and relocation management software: estimated around USD 4.50 billion in 2025 and projected to reach about USD 10.09 billion by 2031, according to Mordor Intelligence.
- Cross-border workforce and migration solutions: estimated around USD 3.8 billion in 2024 and projected to reach about USD 11.6 billion by 2034, according to Global Market Insights.
- Expat relocation services: estimated around USD 4.9 billion in 2024 and projected to reach about USD 11.6 billion by 2034, according to InsightAce Analytic.

Use these as directional market-size estimates, not exact audited numbers.

6.3 How much are people willing to pay to reduce migration-decision risk?

Observed price anchors:
- Initial immigration consultations: often about USD 100-600+, depending on country and lawyer.
- US immigration lawyer rates: commonly USD 100-1,000 per hour depending on complexity and provider.
- Australia immigration lawyer/agent examples: hundreds of dollars for consultation; thousands for visa support.
- UK immigration legal support examples: often several thousand pounds for complex applications.
- Portugal golden visa advisory/legal costs: legal fees can be several thousand euros, with investment minimums and government fees much higher.
- Employee relocation support: often several thousand to tens of thousands per employee; international executive assignments can exceed USD 100,000 in total cost.

Practical B2C willingness-to-pay hypothesis:
- Free: generic country browsing, public rankings, basic checklists.
- USD 19-49: single premium country report or document checklist.
- USD 49-149: personalised country shortlist and risk scan.
- USD 149-499: detailed personal relocation plan with cost/tax/visa comparison.
- USD 500-2,000+: human expert review, lawyer/tax handoff, family relocation planning.
- USD 5,000+: complex visa, investment, business, tax, or full relocation execution.

6.4 Which B2C products already exist for migrants and relocants?

Existing B2C product types:
- Country ranking platforms: Nomad List, Internations rankings, HSBC Expat Explorer, quality-of-life rankings.
- Cost platforms: Numbeo, Expatistan, city cost calculators.
- Expat communities: Expat.com, Internations, Facebook groups, Telegram channels, Reddit communities.
- Visa information sites: government portals, lawyer blogs, visa-agency pages.
- Relocation guides: paid PDFs, checklists, courses, YouTube memberships.
- Digital nomad services: visa guides, coworking/community directories, accommodation platforms.
- Second-passport services: citizenship-by-descent firms, investment migration firms, genealogy/document firms.
- Tax-residency advisers and offshore-planning consultancies.
- AI/legal assistants and intake tools, usually still requiring human professional review.

Gap:
Most B2C products either rank countries broadly, explain one visa route, or provide community anecdotes. Very few combine personal eligibility, legal freshness, costs, tax risk, safety, and verified lived experience into one decision workflow.

6.5 Which B2B products exist for companies, HR, and relocation departments?

Existing B2B categories:
- Global mobility platforms.
- Immigration compliance software.
- Relocation management dashboards.
- Assignment management tools.
- Employee relocation vendors.
- Tax equalisation and payroll compliance tools.
- Employer-of-record platforms.
- Immigration law firm portals.
- Corporate housing and destination services.

Examples/categories:
- Jobbatical positions itself as a global mobility platform for immigration and relocation.
- Gartner reviews a global mobility services category covering vendors that support corporate mobility.
- Large relocation providers offer home search, moving, visa processing, school search, language/cultural training, and assignment management.

B2B buyer pain:
Companies do not only need “where can this employee move?” They need compliance, cost control, risk visibility, employee experience, document tracking, and policy consistency.

6.6 Can the platform monetise personal analysis and recommendations rather than raw information?

Answer: Yes. This is probably the best monetisation path.

Why:
- Raw information is increasingly free but fragmented.
- Official rules are authoritative but hard to interpret.
- Blogs are readable but often outdated.
- Lawyers are expensive and route-specific.
- Users do not know which expert they need until they understand their options.
- Personalised decision support sits before legal execution and can route users to the correct professional.

Product model:
- Free discovery: country database and rough filters.
- Paid assessment: personal country shortlist + risk report.
- Premium report: full comparison, costs, timelines, legal routes, tax warnings, and action plan.
- Expert marketplace: paid handoff to lawyer, tax adviser, relocation agent, school consultant, housing agent.
- Subscription: law-change alerts, document tracker, renewal reminders, community access.
- B2B tier: employee eligibility pre-checks, country risk dashboards, HR policy templates, compliance tracking.

High-value paid deliverables:
- “Best 5 countries for your exact profile.”
- “Fastest legal route to EU residence/citizenship.”
- “Tax-risk comparison for remote worker/founder.”
- “Family relocation plan with schools, healthcare, and citizenship timeline.”
- “Second passport route assessment.”
- “Move-or-wait decision report after law change/election.”

Conclusion
People already pay heavily for migration-related decisions. The strongest product opportunity is a paid personal decision layer that sits between free content and expensive lawyers/agencies.
