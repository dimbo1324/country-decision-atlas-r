4. SEARCH DEMAND AND ONLINE INTEREST
Research date: 16 June 2026

4.1 Is search demand for moving abroad rising?

Answer: There are strong public indicators of rising or spiking search demand, but a complete answer requires exported Google Trends / SEO-tool data for each query and country.

Evidence found:
- Google Trends is the correct primary tool for query-level search interest by time and geography.
- Public reporting after the 2024 U.S. election showed large spikes in searches such as “move to Canada,” “emigrate to Australia,” and general moving-abroad queries.
- Public reports also show large growth in “digital nomad visa” searches in 2024.

Caution:
Search spikes do not equal completed migration. Search interest is an early signal, not proof of actual relocation.

Sources: Google Trends; Sky News; Expat Network. [S27][S28][S29]

4.2 Which migration-related queries are growing fastest?

Likely fast-growing query clusters:
- “digital nomad visa”
- “remote work visa”
- “residence permit”
- “fast residency”
- “easy citizenship”
- “citizenship by descent”
- “second passport”
- “low tax countries”
- “tax residency”
- “golden visa alternatives”
- “move abroad after election”
- “best countries to live”
- “countries with good healthcare”
- “safe countries to move to”

Evidence:
Digital-nomad visa search-demand reporting shows very large growth, and policy reality supports the trend: EY counted more than 48 jurisdictions with digital-nomad/remote-work visa schemes by early 2026.

Sources: Google Trends; EY; Expat Network. [S27][S14][S29]

4.3 Which countries are most often searched with moving abroad?

Based on public search reports and broader migration demand, the main search destinations include:
- Canada.
- Australia.
- New Zealand.
- United Kingdom.
- Spain.
- Portugal.
- Germany.
- France.
- Japan.
- Italy.
- Switzerland.
- Mexico.
- Thailand.
- United Arab Emirates.
- Uruguay, Argentina, and other Latin American destinations in some niche communities.

Examples:
- Post-U.S.-election search spikes focused strongly on Canada and Australia.
- Digital-nomad searches rose around Italy after the launch of Italy's digital-nomad visa.
- Portugal and Spain continue to be heavily searched lifestyle and remote-worker destinations, but face housing, tax, and policy concerns.

Sources: Sky News; Expat Network; Google Trends should be used for confirmation by query/country. [S28][S29][S27]

4.4 Which countries are often compared?

Likely high-intent comparison pairs:
- Portugal vs Spain.
- Canada vs Australia.
- Australia vs New Zealand.
- Germany vs Netherlands.
- Spain vs Italy.
- Uruguay vs Argentina.
- Mexico vs Costa Rica.
- Dubai/UAE vs Singapore.
- Portugal vs Greece.
- Canada vs United States.

Interpretation:
Comparison searches are high-value because they indicate decision-stage users. A platform should prioritise “country A vs country B” pages with structured legal, tax, cost, safety, healthcare, school, and citizenship comparisons.

Method note:
These pairs should be validated with Google Trends, Google Search Console, Ahrefs, Semrush, Reddit search, YouTube search, and autocomplete data.

4.6 Which fears appear in search behavior?

Common fear clusters:
- Crime and personal safety.
- Corruption and bureaucracy.
- Racism/xenophobia.
- Visa refusal.
- War and political risk.
- Losing tax residency control.
- Double taxation.
- Healthcare access failure.
- School integration problems.
- Housing scams.
- Bank-account denial.
- Loneliness and cultural adaptation.
- Regret after moving.

Platform implication:
Fear-based searches are monetisable if the product gives a clear risk score and practical mitigation steps.

4.7 Is interest in digital-nomad visas rising?

Answer: Yes.

Evidence:
- EY counted more than 40 jurisdictions offering digital-nomad/remote-work visa schemes by mid-2024 and more than 48 by early 2026.
- MBO Partners estimates U.S. digital nomads rose to 18.5 million in 2025, up 153% since 2019.
- Japan introduced a six-month digital-nomad status in 2024.
- Thailand expanded long-stay options including the Destination Thailand Visa, with 180-day stays and multi-year validity.

Sources: EY; MBO Partners; Japan MOFA; Reuters. [S14][S13][S15][S16]

4.8 Is interest rising in countries with fast residence, permanent residence, or citizenship?

Answer: Yes, especially among remote workers, weak-passport holders, founders, investors, politically anxious users, and families.

Search themes:
- “fast residency”
- “easy permanent residence”
- “citizenship by descent”
- “citizenship by investment”
- “golden visa alternatives”
- “naturalisation timeline by country”

Reality check:
The fastest routes are not always the safest or best. Some are legally fragile, expensive, or politically controversial. A platform should separate “fast” from “stable,” “cheap,” “family-friendly,” and “citizenship-realistic.”

Sources: Henley & Partners; Spain/Malta reports; Google Trends framework. [S30][S22][S23][S27]
