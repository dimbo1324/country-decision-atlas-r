9. OPPORTUNITIES FOR A MIGRATION DECISION PLATFORM
Research date: 16 June 2026

9.1 Which relocation questions cannot be solved quickly with existing platforms?

Hard questions:
- Which country can I legally move to with my passport, job, income, savings, family, and risk tolerance?
- Which visa route gives me the highest chance of approval?
- Which route leads to permanent residence or citizenship, not just a temporary stay?
- What changed in the law recently?
- Is the online advice I found still valid?
- How much money do I really need for the first 6-12 months?
- What is my rejection risk?
- What documents will slow me down?
- Can my spouse work?
- Can my children access school and healthcare?
- Will I become tax resident?
- What hidden costs are likely?
- What happens if the country tightens rules during my application?

9.2 Which data is scattered across different websites and could be centralised?

Data to centralise:
- Visa routes and eligibility.
- Official fees.
- Processing times.
- Required documents.
- Work rights.
- Spouse/dependant rights.
- Permanent residence and citizenship timelines.
- Tax residency rules.
- Double-tax treaties.
- Cost of living and rent.
- Housing availability.
- Salaries and job-market demand.
- Healthcare access and insurance.
- School access and costs.
- Safety/crime and political risk.
- Banking and proof-of-address rules.
- User case timelines and real costs.
- Law-change history.
- Search and social interest signals.
- Destination sentiment and complaint themes.

9.3 What functions are missing from existing platforms?

Missing or weak functions:
- Personal country matching based on legal eligibility, not lifestyle only.
- Law tracking with dated official sources.
- Rejection-risk scoring.
- Family feasibility scoring.
- Tax-residency risk warnings.
- Country Direction Index.
- Country Interest Index.
- User-case verification.
- Structured “A vs B” country comparisons.
- Law-change alerts.
- Scenario modelling: single / couple / family / founder / remote worker / student.
- Budget model with first-year cash requirement.
- Pathway-to-citizenship calculator.
- Expert handoff when risk is high.

Competitor context:
- Nomads.com is strong for lifestyle/digital-nomad city rankings.
- Numbeo is strong for cost-of-living and quality-of-life crowd data.
- Expat.com is strong for community and general expat guides.
- Telegram, Reddit, YouTube, and Facebook are strong for lived experience.
- None of these is a complete evidence-backed personal migration decision system.

Sources: Nomads.com; Numbeo; Expat.com; community observations. [S32][S33][S34]

9.4 Can you create a Country Interest Index?

Answer: Yes.

Suggested inputs:
1. Google Trends query growth.
2. SEO keyword volume for “move to X,” “visa X,” “citizenship X.”
3. Social community growth: Reddit, Telegram, Facebook, YouTube.
4. Migration stock and flow data.
5. Visa application counts where public.
6. Appointment availability/wait times.
7. Government announcements of new visas.
8. News mentions.
9. Influencer/content volume.
10. Property/rental search interest.
11. Lawyer/agency ad competition.
12. Flight search or relocation service demand, if accessible.

Index output:
- Current interest score.
- 3-month momentum.
- 12-month momentum.
- Source-market breakdown.
- Segment breakdown: students, remote workers, skilled workers, investors, families.
- Confidence level.

9.5 Can you create a Country Direction Index?

Answer: Yes.

Suggested inputs:
- Visa openings/tightenings.
- Permanent residence changes.
- Citizenship changes.
- Student/dependant rules.
- Work-permit thresholds.
- Tax-residency changes.
- Special tax-regime changes.
- Housing restrictions.
- Banking/KYC changes.
- Political rights and rule-of-law indicators.
- Anti-immigration rhetoric and election risk.
- Bureaucratic digitisation.
- Safety/conflict risk.
- Healthcare/school accessibility.

Index output:
- More open / more closed.
- More pro-talent / less pro-talent.
- More pro-business / less pro-business.
- More family-friendly / less family-friendly.
- More stable / more volatile.
- More tax-attractive / less tax-attractive.

9.6 Can you predict rising interest before it becomes obvious?

Answer: Yes, probabilistically.

Early signals:
- Search spikes before official migration data changes.
- New visa announcements.
- Viral YouTube/TikTok/Telegram content.
- Political crises or election shocks.
- Currency collapse or inflation.
- Housing and appointment bottlenecks.
- Increased lawyer/agency advertising.
- Airline/flight search growth.
- Diaspora-community activity.
- Changes in tax or citizenship laws.
- High-net-worth migration reports.
- Refugee/asylum application increases.

Prediction method:
Combine weak signals into an “early demand alert” and compare them against historical cases such as Ukraine 2022, Lebanon crisis, U.S. election search spikes, digital-nomad visa launches, and Canada/UK/Australia student policy changes.

9.7 What data helps users decide faster and more confidently?

Minimum useful dataset:
1. Legal eligibility.
2. Best visa routes.
3. Approval/rejection risk.
4. Timeline.
5. Required documents.
6. First-year cost estimate.
7. Tax-residency warning.
8. Job/income feasibility.
9. Housing reality.
10. Healthcare and insurance.
11. School/childcare if relevant.
12. Safety and political risk.
13. Permanent residence/citizenship route.
14. Recent law changes.
15. Official sources.
16. Similar user cases.

9.8 What should the user receive after using the platform?

Ideal output package:
- Top 3-7 country shortlist.
- Country fit score.
- Legal pathway map.
- Timeline to visa, residence, PR, and citizenship.
- Document checklist.
- Cost estimate for first 3, 6, and 12 months.
- Tax-residency risk summary.
- Family feasibility report.
- Job/business feasibility score.
- Housing risk score.
- Safety and healthcare/school summary.
- Country direction summary.
- Official-source bibliography.
- Next best action: DIY, lawyer call, employer route, study route, defer, or avoid.

9.9 What must be evidence-based rather than subjective?

Must be evidence-based:
- Visa eligibility.
- Fees.
- Processing times, when official or statistically measured.
- Required documents.
- Work rights.
- Spouse/dependant rights.
- PR/citizenship timeline.
- Tax residency rules.
- Healthcare eligibility.
- School eligibility.
- Criminal/safety statistics.
- Official inflation/unemployment data.
- Law changes.
- Refusal statistics where available.

Can be subjective but should be labelled:
- Lifestyle fit.
- Friendliness.
- Social acceptance.
- Food/culture.
- “Feels safe.”
- Bureaucracy frustration.
- Dating/social life.
- Weather preference.
- Community quality.

9.10 What features would make the platform unique rather than a copy of Nomad List, Numbeo, Expat.com, or Telegram?

Unique feature set:
1. Personal eligibility engine.
2. Evidence-backed legal route comparison.
3. Law-change tracker with official sources.
4. Country Interest Index.
5. Country Direction Index.
6. Rejection-risk model.
7. Family migration feasibility score.
8. Tax-residency risk warnings.
9. Verified user case database.
10. “Country A vs Country B” decision pages.
11. First-year relocation budget estimator.
12. PR/citizenship timeline simulator.
13. Expert escalation for high-risk answers.
14. Confidence score for every recommendation.
15. Alerts when a user's chosen pathway changes.
16. Community data converted into structured insights.

Positioning statement:
“Not another country guide. A personal migration decision engine that combines law, cost, risk, trends, and real cases.”
