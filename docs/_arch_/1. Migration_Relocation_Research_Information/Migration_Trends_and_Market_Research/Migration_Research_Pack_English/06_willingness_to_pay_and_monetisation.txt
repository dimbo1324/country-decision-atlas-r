6. WILLINGNESS TO PAY AND MONETISATION
Research date: 16 June 2026

6.1 What migration information are people already willing to pay for?

People already pay for:
- Immigration lawyer consultations.
- Visa eligibility assessments.
- Full visa application preparation.
- Document review.
- Translation and apostille support.
- Tax-residency advice.
- Company formation and freelancer registration.
- Relocation packages.
- Housing search.
- School search.
- Health-insurance setup.
- Bank-account setup.
- Country reports and relocation guides.
- Checklists, templates, and paid communities.
- Second-passport and investment-migration advice.

Conclusion:
Users do not pay for generic information. They pay to reduce uncertainty, avoid expensive mistakes, save time, and receive personalised answers.

6.2 Which paid services are growing fastest?

High-growth service categories:
- Visa tech and immigration-law platforms.
- Global mobility SaaS for HR teams.
- Employer-of-record and remote hiring compliance.
- Relocation management platforms.
- Digital-nomad visa support.
- Tax-residency and cross-border structuring advice.
- Founder/startup visa support.
- Investment migration and private-client residence planning.

Commercial examples:
- Localyze: immigration and relocation automation for companies.
- Envoy Global: immigration services and technology.
- Fragomen: global immigration law and corporate mobility.
- Startup-visa and founder-relocation programs tracked by StartupBlink and similar sources.

Sources: Localyze; Envoy; Fragomen; StartupBlink. [S35][S36][S37][S31]

6.3 How much are people willing to pay to reduce risk?

Market price examples:
- Immigration consultation: often about USD 150-500 per hour, depending on country and adviser.
- Germany relocation support: visa-only services can cost around EUR 1,500-3,000; full family packages can exceed EUR 5,000-10,000.
- Dutch immigration legal packages: examples range from hundreds to more than EUR 1,000 excluding VAT for specific categories.
- UK immigration lawyer fees: often thousands of pounds for family, work, student, or complex matters.
- U.S. immigration lawyer fees: often thousands of dollars for employment, family, or fiancé pathways.
- Australia visa legal support: temporary, skilled, and partner visas can range from several thousand to more than AUD 10,000 depending on complexity.

Interpretation:
A pre-lawyer “country/pathway risk report” priced from roughly USD/EUR 29-299 could be attractive if it helps users avoid spending thousands on the wrong country or wrong route. Premium expert-reviewed reports could justify higher pricing.

Sources: public pricing pages of immigration lawyers and relocation agencies; commercial market reports. Treat as indicative, not universal.

6.4 What B2C products already exist for migrants and relocators?

Existing B2C product types:
- Destination-ranking sites: Nomads.com / Nomad List.
- Cost-of-living databases: Numbeo.
- Expat communities and guides: Expat.com, InterNations, Reddit, Facebook groups.
- Visa eligibility quizzes and agency funnels.
- Relocation checklists.
- Country reports.
- Paid newsletters and communities.
- Lawyer marketplaces.
- Tax-residency consultations.
- Digital-nomad guides.
- AI migration assistants and country-comparison tools.

Gaps:
Most B2C products are either generic content, crowd-sourced lifestyle data, or lead-generation funnels. Few provide structured, personalised, evidence-backed pathway comparison.

Sources: Nomads.com; Numbeo; Expat.com; observed AI relocation tools. [S32][S33][S34]

6.6 Can the platform monetise personalised analysis rather than information itself?

Answer: Yes. This is the strongest monetisation strategy.

Possible paid products:
1. Personal country shortlist.
2. Visa/pathway feasibility report.
3. Rejection-risk and document-risk report.
4. Family relocation feasibility report.
5. Tax-residency risk snapshot.
6. Digital-nomad legality checker.
7. Startup/founder route comparison.
8. Second-passport route analysis.
9. Law-change alerts for selected countries.
10. Expert-reviewed premium report.
11. Lawyer/tax adviser referral marketplace.
12. Company HR dashboard for employees considering relocation.

Pricing logic:
- Free: basic country discovery and public articles.
- Low-ticket: USD/EUR 19-49 for checklists, country profiles, and comparison pages.
- Mid-ticket: USD/EUR 79-299 for personalised AI reports.
- Premium: USD/EUR 500-2,000+ for expert-reviewed packages.
- B2B: subscription per company or per relocating employee.

Key principle:
The user is buying reduced uncertainty, not text.
