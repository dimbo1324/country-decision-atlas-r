# Migration Market and Global Mobility Trends

A consolidated overview of strategic market research (the English-language "Migration Market Research" packages and global-mobility reports). Overlaps thematically with [../migration/](../migration/00-overview-and-sources.md); this file focuses primarily on market sizing, demographics, and strategic takeaways.

## Headline finding

International migration is growing structurally over the long term, but the picture is becoming more selective, politicized, and segmented. It's not "more migration everywhere": several major destinations are tightening student, family, low-wage, and temporary pathways, while simultaneously competing more aggressively for highly skilled workers, founders, investors, healthcare workers, AI talent, and remote workers.

## Market statistics

- International migrants: ~304 million as of mid-2024 (≈3.7–4% of world population). Long-term trend: ~154 million in 1990, ~281 million in 2020, ~302–304 million in 2023–2024.
- Permanent migration into OECD countries: a record ~6.5 million in 2023, then a moderate decline across many OECD countries in 2024.
- The OECD's foreign-born population: over 160 million in 2024; the share grew from 9.1% (2014) to 11.5%.
- Labor migration: the ILO estimates 167.7 million international labor migrants in the workforce in 2022.
- Forced displacement: UNHCR — 123.2 million at the end of 2024; IDMC — 83.4 million internally displaced.
- A gender shift: a 51.1% male / 48.9% female split in 2005; 52% / 48% by 2024. A strong male skew in Western Asia and the GCC (contract labor); women predominate in Oceania, North America, and Europe.

## Regions and countries

- Europe and North America receive the majority of international migrants (more than 50% of the global total). North Africa and Western Asia are major hubs due to Gulf labor migration, refugee flows, and regional conflict.
- Latin America and the Caribbean, along with Africa, recorded the fastest relative growth in migrant stocks since 2020 (regional displacement, economic integration, geopolitics).
- Top origin countries: India, China, Mexico, Ukraine, Russia, Syria, Bangladesh, Venezuela. Top destination countries: the US, Germany, Saudi Arabia, the UK, France, Canada, the UAE, Australia, Russia, Turkey, Spain, Italy.

## The intent to migrate and fast-growing categories

- About 15% of adults worldwide wanted to relocate permanently in 2025 (~900 million), slightly below the 2021–2024 record of 16%. The US remains the most desired destination, but its share among would-be migrants has fallen to a near-two-decade low; Canada, the UK, Germany, and Australia remain "dream countries."
- The fastest-growing categories: humanitarian and asylum-driven migration; temporary labor and sector-specific recruitment (healthcare, care work, construction, logistics, agriculture, services); student migration (though Canada, Australia, and the UK are rebalancing); digital nomadism (becoming more regulated); investment and "wealth" migration (as European golden visas tighten).

## Legal direction: two simultaneous trends

- **Selective openness**: talent visas, startup visas, remote-worker visas, healthcare-worker recruitment, founder programs, AI/tech-specialist pathways.
- **Broad tightening**: student quotas, higher salary thresholds, stricter family reunification, longer settlement timelines, anti-abuse rules, closing real-estate-based golden visas, tighter asylum and irregular-migration controls.

## Willingness to pay and B2B

Users already pay for immigration lawyers, visa agents, relocation consultants, premium reports, checklists, document review, employer-sponsored relocation support, tax-residency consultations, and investment-migration advisors. B2B buyers pay for global mobility SaaS, immigration compliance, relocation dashboards, payroll/EOR, and employee-move vendors. The strongest monetization opportunity isn't "raw" information — it's personalized analysis, eligibility scoring, risk reduction, and implementation support.

## Recommended product positioning

Don't build "another Nomad List" or "another expat forum" — build a decision engine that answers: "Which countries fit my profile?" "Which route is realistic?" "How much will it cost?" "What could go wrong?" "Is the country becoming more open or more closed?" "What evidence backs this recommendation?"

### The minimal evidence stack per country

Visa pathways, eligibility, fees, timelines, renewal rules, permanent-residency rules, citizenship rules, tax residency, healthcare, schools, housing/rent, salaries/jobs, safety/crime, banking, sanctions/AML friction, business registration, cost of living, language requirements, political/legal direction, user reviews, and links to official sources.
