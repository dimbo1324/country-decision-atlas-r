SECTION 1. MARKET SIZE AND TRAJECTORY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1.1 THE NUMBER OF INTERNATIONAL MIGRANTS

  2000s          ~191 million people
  2010           ~221 million people
  2020           ~275 million people
  2024           ~304 million people (3.7% of world population)

  20-year growth: +55–60%
  Source: UN DESA, 2024.

  TAKEAWAY: The market has grown by more than half over two decades.
  Migrants' share of world population is growing slower than the
  absolute numbers (from 2.9% in 1990 to 3.7% in 2024), meaning
  steady structural growth, not an explosion.

1.3 THE MAIN COUNTRIES OF ORIGIN

  The top-5 by diaspora size: India, China, Mexico, Ukraine, Russia.

  • India: the largest diaspora; IT, healthcare, students, the Gulf corridor.
  • China: students, business/investment, skilled workers.
  • Mexico: the traditional corridor into the US.
  • Ukraine: the 2022 war — the largest recent displacement in Europe.
  • Russia: post-Soviet mobility + relocation after 2022.

SECTION 2. THE FASTEST-GROWING DESTINATIONS (2020–2026)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  1. DIGITAL NOMADS
     MBO Partners (2025): 18.5 million American digital nomads,
     +153% growth since 2019.
     Dozens of countries have already launched or are piloting
     dedicated visas.
     The fastest-growing niche segment.

  2. UKRAINE → EU / EUROPE / NORTH AMERICA
     Europe's largest humanitarian crisis since 2022.
     EU temporary protection created a mass movement.

  3. SKILLED PROFESSIONALS → OECD COUNTRIES
     OECD labor migration volume declined in 2024 after the
     post-pandemic peak, but stayed well above 2019 and 2015 levels.

  4. INTERNATIONAL STUDENTS
     UNESCO: international student mobility has tripled over
     20 years — almost 7.3 million students studying abroad.
     The UK, Canada, and Australia tightened requirements →
     growth is redistributing toward Europe and Asia.

  5. FORCED DISPLACEMENT
     UNHCR (end of 2024): 123.2 million forcibly displaced people.
     Conflicts: Sudan, Afghanistan, Venezuela, Myanmar.

  6. INVESTMENT MIGRATION
     Henley (2025): a record millionaire exodus.
     Meanwhile the IMF and the EU are tightening regulation of
     "citizenship/residency by investment" schemes.

SECTION 3. COOLING DESTINATIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  1. IRREGULAR EU ROUTES
     Frontex: -38% illegal EU border crossings in 2024.

  2. THE UK (students and low-skilled workers)
     Closing the student-dependent visa, raising salary
     thresholds, ending overseas care-worker recruitment.

  3. CANADA (broad temporary-resident inflow)
     2026–2028 plans: lower temporary-resident quotas.
     Housing and public-service strain.

  4. AUSTRALIA (uncapped international student inflow)
     2026: managed growth instead of uncapped intake.

  5. SPAIN (a golden visa via real estate)
     April 2025: Spain closed the real-estate investment residency route.

  6. THE US (as the sole dream destination)
     Gallup 2025: only 15% of would-be migrants name the US as
     their top choice — a ~20-year low.

SECTION 4. DESIRE TO MIGRATE — SURVEY DATA
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Per Gallup:
  • Long term: the desire to migrate is rising.
  • 2025 saw a regional dip in Latin America and Africa, but the
    level remains high.
  • 33% of adults in Sub-Saharan Africa want to relocate
    permanently.
  • 25% — Latin America and the Caribbean.

  AGE GROUPS (interest in migration):
  • 15–24: the highest aspirations.
  • 25–34: the strongest actual conversion of interest into action.
  • 35–44: high interest, constrained by life circumstances.
  • 45+: focused on quality of life, retirement, healthcare, taxes.

  PROFESSIONAL GROUPS WITH THE HIGHEST INTEREST:
  IT, software, AI/data • Engineers (electrical, mechanical, energy)
  Medical professionals (doctors, nurses) • Scientists, graduate students
  Finance, consulting • Skilled trades (construction, electricians)
  Entrepreneurs, founders • Freelancers, remote workers

SECTION 5. FORECASTS FOR 2030–2040
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  BASELINE SCENARIO (most likely):
  — Total migrant flows keep growing in absolute numbers.
  — Aging developed economies will compete for skilled workers:
    IT, medicine, engineering, construction, elder care.
  — Digital nomad and remote-work migration becomes more
    regulated: visas, tax residency, social insurance,
    employer rules.
  — Student mobility diversifies: flow shifts away from
    overloaded English-speaking countries.

  PRESSURE SCENARIO (conflict/climate):
  — Wars, failed states, water/food shortages, and climate
    disasters intensify forced migration.
  — World Bank: up to 216 million internal climate migrants
    by 2050 (a pessimistic scenario).

  COMPETITION-FOR-TALENT SCENARIO:
  — Countries open more pathways for healthcare, engineering,
    AI/IT, the green transition, advanced manufacturing, logistics,
    elder care — and tighten pathways for low-skilled workers.

  BACKLASH SCENARIO (stricter rules):
  — Housing crises and political polarization reduce the appeal
    of Canada, the UK, Australia, and parts of the EU → migration
    redistributes toward alternatives.

SECTION 6. COUNTRIES ACTIVELY COMPETING FOR MIGRANTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  STRONG POSITIONS:
  Germany and the EU  — the Opportunity Card + Blue Card reform
  UAE/Saudi Arabia    — long-term residency, zero taxes,
                         mega-projects
  Singapore           — high-skill, tech, finance
  Portugal            — lifestyle, the Digital Nomad Visa (with limits)
  Spain               — lifestyle, EU access

  GAINING GROUND (alternative hubs):
  Malaysia, Thailand, Indonesia — digital nomads, lifestyle
  Georgia, Armenia, Serbia      — post-Soviet relocation
  Uruguay, Costa Rica, Panama   — stability, Latin America
  Mexico                        — proximity, lifestyle, remote work
  Japan, South Korea            — starting to open pragmatically

  LOSING APPEAL (for specific segments):
  Canada — for lower-income students and temporary residents
  The UK — for lower/mid-income workers
  Australia — for the marginal student segment
  Spain — for real-estate investors (the golden visa is closed)
  The US — for a portion of would-be migrants (policy instability)
  Gulf states — for those seeking genuine long-term status
