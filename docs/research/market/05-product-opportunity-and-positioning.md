SECTION 1. THE RESEARCH'S HEADLINE FINDING
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  The strongest opportunity isn't "yet another country-ranking site."

  The market already has:
  • Cost-of-living data (Numbeo, Expatistan, Mercer)
  • City rankings for nomads (Nomads.com)
  • Visa summaries (VisaGuide.World, law firms)
  • Expat stories (Expat.com, InterNations, Reddit, YouTube)
  • Institutional indicators (OECD, World Bank, UN, IOM)

  WHAT'S MISSING:
  These sources rarely become a DECISION-MAKING SYSTEM. They
  provide information, but don't answer the question:

  "Given my passport, income, profession, family, savings,
  risk tolerance, desired lifestyle, tax situation, and long-term
  citizenship goal — which country actually fits me? And what has
  changed recently that could make my decision wrong?"

SECTION 2. THE PRODUCT FORMULA IN ONE SENTENCE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Build a "Country Decision OS" — a platform that combines
  official data, legal-change tracking, a country's trend, real
  cost of living, user stories, expert review, AI summaries, and
  scenario modeling into one transparent decision-support system.

SECTION 3. THE PLATFORM'S FIVE LAYERS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  LAYER 1: COUNTRY CARDS
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Structured country profiles with objective indicators. Every
  figure carries a source, a verification date, a confidence
  level. Not just "country X scores 7/10," but:
  "Freedom House freedom rating: 84/100. Source: FH 2024.
  Verified: March 2025. Trend: ↓ since 2021."

  LAYER 2: THE LEGAL CHANGE TRACKER
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Visas, residency, citizenship, taxes, property, business,
  and family rules — every item with a timestamp and a source
  link. Legal signals: what changed, what's changing, and its
  impact level on different user groups.

  LAYER 3: THE COUNTRY DIRECTION INDEX
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A 5–10-year trend based on laws, indices, and economic
  indicators. Shows whether a country is becoming:
  • more open/closed
  • more liberal/authoritarian
  • more pro-business/bureaucratic
  • more expensive/affordable
  • more stable/risky
  • more friendly/hostile to migrants

  LAYER 4: THE SCENARIO SIMULATOR (THE DECISION ENGINE)
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Personalized country fit based on: budget, passport, profession,
  family status, income type, tax exposure, and time horizon.
  Output: a ranking + a score + an explanation + strengths +
  weaknesses + risk warnings + a breakdown + confidence + sources.

  LAYER 5: THE EXPERIENCE LAYER
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Moderated real stories, warnings, post-arrival checklists,
  cost reports, verified community advice. Structured cases
  tagged by: passport, route, city, budget, family status,
  profession, outcome.

SECTION 5. THE LONG-TERM COMPETITIVE MOAT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  The moat won't come from the UI itself, but from:
  • A structured, sourced country database
  • An evidence layer (proof behind every claim)
  • Legal signals with a change history
  • Structured user stories
  • Data-quality control
  • A translation layer
  • An explainable decision engine
  • Historical change tracking

  WHAT MOST COMPETITORS ARE MISSING:
  × the link from law → impact → user scenario
  × country direction
  × source-backed decisions
  × structured migration stories
  × contradiction detection
  × scenario-specific scoring
  × a trust/confidence layer

SECTION 8. THE PRODUCT'S CORE STANCE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  The platform does NOT say: "This country is the best."

  The platform says:
  "For your scenario, this country looks stronger on these
  criteria, weaker on these, carries these risks, is backed by
  these sources, and part of the data needs careful verification."

  THE PRODUCT IS A RISK-REDUCTION SYSTEM for irreversible life
  decisions, not just a list of countries.
