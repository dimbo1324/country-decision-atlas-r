# Migration and Country-Choice Research — Overview, Methodology, and Sources

This is a consolidated overview of research spanning 120 questions about international migration, relocation, and country choice. The material was consolidated from English-language research packages (which duplicated each other extensively) into a single set of thematic documents. References like `[S01]` point to the sources listed at the end of this file.

## The research's headline finding

The market doesn't need another "best countries to move to" survey blog. It needs a **trust layer on top of scattered relocation information**.

> People need a verifiable, current, personalized, and comparable decision system: how to choose a country, understand legal pathways, estimate real costs, and recognize shifts in political and legal direction.

## Key user personas

1. **The budget relocator** — affordability, legal stay, basic safety.
2. **The skilled professional** — a work pathway, salary, permanent residency, diploma recognition.
3. **The student** — study + post-study work + a path to permanent residency.
4. **The family** — safety, schools, healthcare, long-term stability.
5. **The digital nomad** — legal remote work, tax clarity, cost, internet, community.
6. **The entrepreneur** — company registration, banking, taxes, market access, a founder residency.
7. **The investor / HNWI** — diversification, taxes, a portfolio of passports/residencies, safety.
8. **The politically motivated migrant** — a safe legal exit, documents, risk planning.
9. **The retiree** — healthcare, climate, cost of living, safety, residency.

## The data layers a strong platform must integrate

A country profile; visa and residency pathways; a permanent-residency/citizenship timeline; an official-source tracker; a legal-change tracker; cost of living and rent; a tax model; a healthcare/school model; labor-market fit; safety and crime; political and legal direction; community evidence; expert validation; a user-scenario simulator; a post-relocation checklist.

## The source reliability model

- **A** — an official source: government / law / court / official statistics.
- **B** — an international organization: OECD, the UN, IOM, the World Bank, ILO.
- **C** — a recognized legal/tax/mobility firm, dated and attributed.
- **D** — a commercial platform with a transparent methodology.
- **E** — public personal community experience.
- **F** — an unverified rumor / an anonymous claim / an old forum post.

Community content can't be equated with law, but it's extremely valuable for surfacing hidden problems.

## "Country attractiveness" is not one score

A single score is dangerous. It's better to use a set of scores: legal feasibility; financial feasibility; career/business fit; family fit; safety/stability; adaptation difficulty; long-term status; risk of rule changes; the community's "pain level"; a confidence level. The user should be able to reweight them.
